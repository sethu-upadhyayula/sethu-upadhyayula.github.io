About Me

I am Sethu Madhav Upadhyayula, and I studied Mathematics and Computing at IIT Delhi, where I completed both my undergraduate and postgraduate degrees. My master's thesis focused on the structural controllability of networks, drawing on ideas from graph theory and matrix theory.

After graduating, I began my career in software engineering at Oracle, where I worked on banking applications, microservices, and related infrastructure using technologies such as Java, Spring Boot, and JavaScript.

Over time, I developed a growing interest in financial markets and quantitative finance, eventually making the transition into quantitative roles within the banking industry. At ANZ (Australia and New Zealand Banking Group Limited), I worked across multiple asset classes, building analytics and reporting tools and working closely with trading and risk teams. More recently, I joined the Quantitative Research team at JPMC (J.P. Morgan Chase & Co.), where my work has been focused on loan modeling within the credit business.

Outside work, chess has been a constant part of my life for many years. I represented my university chess team and served as its captain for a year. Beyond competitive play, I am also a licensed national arbiter and enjoy endgames, studies, compositions, and exploring some of the less conventional corners of the game.

Biology and music are more recent interests of mine, picked up from my life partner, who holds a master's degree in biotechnology and has been learning Carnatic music for over a decade.
