2. Prokaryotic Cell Structure and Function

Prokaryotic cell walls and membranes were covered in Chapter 1 of Cell Biology; this chapter covers the additional external structures many bacteria use for movement, attachment, protection, and survival.

Flagella and Pili

Definition - Bacterial Flagella and Pili

Bacterial flagella are rotary propulsion structures, rotated by a motor embedded in the cell envelope, and are structurally and mechanistically entirely distinct from eukaryotic flagella (Chapter 5 of Cell Biology). Pili (or fimbriae) are shorter, non-motile appendages used for surface attachment and, in the case of the specialized sex pilus, for DNA transfer during conjugation (Chapter 6 of Genetics).

Bacterial Chemotaxis

Directed bacterial movement toward favourable and away from unfavourable chemical gradients - chemotaxis - was covered in detail in Chapter 4 of Cell Signaling & Cancer Biology, including both the flagellar rotation mechanism and its underlying two-component-like signaling pathway.

Capsules

Definition - Bacterial Capsule

A capsule is a well-organized, often polysaccharide-based layer external to the cell wall, distinguished from a more diffuse "slime layer" by its more defined structure. Capsules protect against desiccation and, importantly, against phagocytosis (Chapter 1 of Immunology) by the host immune system, making capsule production a significant virulence factor for several important bacterial pathogens.

Cell Inclusions: Endospores and Gas Vesicles

Example - The Bacterial Endospore

Endospores, formed by some bacterial genera (e.g., Bacillus, Clostridium) under nutrient-limiting conditions, are extraordinarily resistant dormant structures capable of surviving heat, desiccation, radiation, and chemical disinfection far beyond what the vegetative cell could tolerate - a major consideration in sterilization (Chapter 4). Gas vesicles, found in some aquatic bacteria and archaea, are gas-filled protein structures that provide buoyancy, allowing cells to position themselves at a preferred depth in the water column.
