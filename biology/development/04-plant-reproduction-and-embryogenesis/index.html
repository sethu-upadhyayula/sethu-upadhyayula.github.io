4. Plant Reproduction and Embryogenesis

Plant development follows a fundamentally different reproductive strategy from animals, built around an alternation of generations and a distinctive double fertilization event unique to flowering plants.

Male and Female Gametophyte Development

Definition - Gametophyte

Unlike animals, plants alternate between a diploid sporophyte generation and a haploid gametophyte generation. In flowering plants, the male gametophyte (pollen grain) develops within the anther, while the female gametophyte (embryo sac) develops within the ovule - both highly reduced compared to the free-living gametophyte generation seen in bryophytes (Chapter 6 of Diversity, Taxonomy & Phylogenetics).

Double Fertilization

The Double Fertilization Event

A distinctive feature of angiosperm reproduction: a pollen tube delivers two sperm cells to the embryo sac. One sperm fertilizes the egg cell, forming the diploid zygote; the second fuses with two polar nuclei to form the triploid endosperm, a nutritive tissue that will nourish the developing embryo - meaning a single pollination event simultaneously initiates both the embryo and its food supply.

Establishment of Embryo Symmetry

Following fertilization, the plant zygote undergoes an asymmetric first division that establishes the basic apical-basal axis of the future embryo, setting up the polarity from which the shoot and root poles (Chapter 6) will later develop - conceptually similar to the cytoplasmic-determinant-driven asymmetries of animal cleavage (Chapter 3), though achieved through different molecular machinery.

Seed Formation and Germination

Remark - Seed Dormancy and Germination

As the embryo and endosperm mature, the surrounding ovule develops into a desiccation-tolerant seed (Chapter 6 of Diversity, Taxonomy & Phylogenetics), often entering a dormant state that delays germination until environmental conditions (moisture, temperature, sometimes light) are favourable - a developmental checkpoint with no direct animal parallel, allowing plants to time the resumption of development to their environment.
