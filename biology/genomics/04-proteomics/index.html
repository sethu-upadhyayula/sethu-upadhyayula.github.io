4. Proteomics

The transcriptome only partially predicts a cell's actual protein content, since translation efficiency, post-translational modification, and protein turnover (Chapter 4 of Molecular Biology) all intervene between mRNA abundance and functional protein levels - motivating the direct, large-scale study of proteins themselves.

Mass Spectrometry-Based Proteomics

Definition - Shotgun Proteomics

The dominant modern approach digests a complex protein mixture into peptides (typically using the enzyme trypsin), separates them by liquid chromatography (Chapter 9 of Biochemistry), and identifies them by tandem mass spectrometry, matching the resulting fragmentation patterns against a database of predicted peptides from a reference genome - allowing thousands of proteins to be identified and quantified from a single sample.

Post-Translational Modification Mapping

Beyond simply cataloging which proteins are present, mass-spectrometry-based proteomics can map specific post-translational modifications - phosphorylation sites, ubiquitination, glycosylation (Chapter 4 of Molecular Biology) - across the entire proteome, revealing which signaling pathways (Chapters 1–2 of Cell Signaling) are active in a given cellular state.

Protein Interaction Mapping

Example - Mapping the Interactome

Techniques such as yeast two-hybrid screening and affinity purification coupled to mass spectrometry systematically identify which proteins physically interact with one another, building an interactome - a network map of protein-protein interactions across the whole proteome - that complements the sequence- and structure-based function prediction methods covered in Chapter 6 of Structural & Computational Biology.

Quantitative Proteomics

Comparing protein abundance across conditions - analogous to differential gene expression analysis (Chapter 3) but at the protein level - helps identify which regulatory changes detected transcriptionally actually translate into altered protein output, an important check given that mRNA and protein abundance often correlate only moderately for any individual gene.
