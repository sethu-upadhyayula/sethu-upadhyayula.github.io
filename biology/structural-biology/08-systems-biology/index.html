8. Systems Biology

Rather than studying one gene, protein, or pathway in isolation, systems biology treats biological processes as networks of interacting components, using mathematical modelling and large-scale data to understand behaviour that emerges only at the level of the whole system.

Modelling Metabolic Pathways and Disease

Definition - Systems Biology

An approach that integrates genomic, transcriptomic, proteomic, and metabolomic data (Chapter 3 of Genomics) into quantitative models of cellular networks - for example, modelling an entire metabolic pathway's flux under different conditions, rather than studying one enzyme's kinetics alone (Chapter 4 of Biochemistry) - with the goal of predicting how a system responds to perturbation, whether a genetic mutation, a drug, or a change in environment.

Data Science Applications in Biology and Drug Discovery

Machine learning and statistical methods are now applied broadly across biology and medicine - from prioritizing candidate genes in disease studies, to the structure-prediction and drug-design applications covered in Chapters 3 and 7, to integrating multiple heterogeneous data types (genomic, imaging, clinical) into a single predictive model.

Digital Health and Personalized Medicine

Remark - Personalized Medicine

Combining an individual's genomic and molecular data with computational models is the basis of personalized medicine - tailoring diagnosis, prognosis, and treatment choice to a specific patient rather than a population average, for example by predicting which cancer patients are likely to respond to a particular targeted therapy (Chapter 6 of Cell Signaling & Cancer Biology) based on their tumor's specific mutations.
