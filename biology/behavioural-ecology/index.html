Behavioural Ecology

Behavioural ecology studies animal behaviour through an evolutionary lens - asking not just how a behaviour works mechanistically, but why natural selection has favoured it. This subject covers the foundational distinction between proximate and ultimate explanations, and surveys the major categories of behaviour: foraging, communication, migration, mating, sociality, and conflict.
