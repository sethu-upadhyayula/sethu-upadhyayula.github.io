1. Cell Wall and Plasma Membrane Structure

Every cell is bounded by a plasma membrane, and many are further enclosed by a rigid cell wall. Together these structures define the cell's boundary, protect it, and mediate everything that crosses in or out.

The Fluid Mosaic Model

Definition - Fluid Mosaic Model

The plasma membrane is a phospholipid bilayer in which proteins float and diffuse laterally, like icebergs in a fluid sea. Membrane fluidity is tuned by lipid composition - unsaturated fatty acid tails and cholesterol (in eukaryotes) prevent overly tight packing at physiological temperature.

Membrane proteins fall into two broad classes: integral proteins, embedded in or spanning the bilayer (often via α-helical transmembrane domains), and peripheral proteins, loosely associated with the membrane surface. Integral proteins serve as channels, transporters, receptors, and enzymes; the specific complement of proteins in a membrane determines its function far more than the lipid bilayer itself, which is largely similar across cell types.

Prokaryotic versus Eukaryotic Membranes

Bacterial and archaeal membranes differ chemically - bacteria use ester-linked fatty acid chains, while archaea use ether-linked isoprenoid chains, one of several features distinguishing the three domains of life.

The Cell Wall

Example - Cell Wall Composition Across Kingdoms

Bacterial cell walls are built from peptidoglycan (a mesh of sugar chains cross-linked by short peptides), the target of penicillin and the basis of the Gram stain distinction between thick-walled Gram-positive and thin-walled, outer-membrane-bearing Gram-negative bacteria. Fungal cell walls are made of chitin, while plant cell walls are built primarily from cellulose (Chapter 1 of Biochemistry) reinforced with hemicellulose, pectin, and, in woody tissue, lignin.

Remark - Why Animal Cells Lack a Wall

Animal cells rely instead on an internal cytoskeleton (Chapter 5) and an external extracellular matrix for mechanical support, allowing the shape flexibility needed for migration and tissue remodelling that a rigid wall would prevent.
