3. The Nucleus, Endoplasmic Reticulum and Golgi Apparatus

These three organelles form a continuous, cooperative system in eukaryotic cells: the nucleus stores and transcribes genetic information, the endoplasmic reticulum synthesizes and folds proteins and lipids, and the Golgi apparatus sorts and dispatches the ER's products to their final destinations.

The Nucleus

Nuclear Envelope and Nuclear Pores

The nucleus is bounded by a double membrane, the nuclear envelope, perforated by nuclear pore complexes that regulate the selective, energy-dependent transport of proteins and RNA between the nucleus and cytoplasm. Within the nucleus, the nucleolus is the site of ribosomal RNA synthesis and ribosome subunit assembly.

The Endoplasmic Reticulum

The rough ER, studded with ribosomes, is the site of synthesis for secreted and membrane proteins, which are co-translationally translocated into the ER lumen or membrane and undergo initial folding and glycosylation. The smooth ER lacks ribosomes and instead specializes in lipid synthesis, steroid hormone production, and, in liver cells, detoxification of drugs and xenobiotics.

Protein Targeting and Trafficking

Definition - Signal Sequences

Proteins destined for the secretory pathway carry a short N-terminal signal sequence recognized by the signal recognition particle (SRP), which directs the ribosome to the ER membrane. Similar targeting sequences direct proteins to mitochondria, chloroplasts, the nucleus, and peroxisomes, each read by a distinct receptor/translocon system.

The Golgi Apparatus

Proteins and lipids leaving the ER pass through the Golgi's stack of flattened cisternae (cis, medial, and trans), where they undergo further processing - most notably additional glycosylation and proteolytic maturation - and are sorted into vesicles addressed to the plasma membrane, lysosomes, or secretory granules.

Remark - Vesicular Transport

Movement between these compartments occurs via coated vesicles (COPI, COPII, and clathrin coats), whose budding and fusion is directed by small GTPases (Rab proteins) and SNARE proteins that ensure each vesicle fuses only with its correct target membrane.
