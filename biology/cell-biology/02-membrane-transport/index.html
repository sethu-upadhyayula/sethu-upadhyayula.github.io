2. Membrane Transport and Membrane Potential

Because the hydrophobic core of the bilayer blocks most polar and charged solutes, cells rely on a set of specialized transport mechanisms to move material across the membrane in a controlled way.

Passive Transport

Diffusion and Osmosis

Simple diffusion allows small, nonpolar molecules (O₂, CO₂) to cross the bilayer directly, moving down their concentration gradient. Osmosis is the diffusion of water itself across a semipermeable membrane, from a region of lower solute concentration to higher, and underlies the swelling or shrinking of cells placed in hypotonic or hypertonic solutions.

Facilitated diffusion uses membrane proteins - channels or carriers - to move polar solutes down their concentration gradient without energy input. Ion channels are typically gated (voltage-gated, ligand-gated, or mechanically gated) and highly selective for a particular ion.

Active Transport

Definition - Active Transport

Movement of a solute against its concentration gradient, requiring energy input. Primary active transport (e.g., the Na⁺/K⁺-ATPase) uses ATP hydrolysis directly. Secondary active transport uses the electrochemical gradient of one ion (built up by primary transport) to drive the uphill movement of another solute, via symporters or antiporters.

Membrane Potential and Action Potentials

Unequal distribution of ions across the plasma membrane, maintained by pumps and selective channel permeability, produces a voltage difference - the membrane potential. In neurons and other excitable cells, this resting potential can be rapidly and transiently reversed by the sequential opening of voltage-gated Na⁺ and K⁺ channels, producing the action potential that propagates electrical signals along the cell.

Remark - The Nernst Equation

The equilibrium potential for a single ion species is given by the Nernst equation, $E_{ion} = \frac{RT}{zF}\ln\frac{[\text{ion}]_{out}}{[\text{ion}]_{in}}$, the voltage at which the electrical and chemical driving forces on that ion exactly balance.
