1. Cell Surface Receptors and Signal Transduction

Most signaling molecules - hormones, growth factors, neurotransmitters - cannot cross the plasma membrane. Instead, cells detect them using surface receptors that relay the signal inward through a chain of intracellular events, a process broadly called signal transduction.

Hormones and Their Receptors

Definition - Ligand and Receptor

A signaling molecule (the ligand) binds with high specificity and affinity to its receptor, a protein whose conformation changes upon binding, initiating a response. Water-soluble hormones (e.g., insulin, adrenaline) act through cell-surface receptors, while lipid-soluble hormones (e.g., steroid hormones) can cross the membrane and bind intracellular receptors directly, which then act as transcription factors.

Classes of Cell Surface Receptor

Major Receptor Families
  • Ion channel-linked receptors - ligand binding directly opens an ion channel (e.g., the nicotinic acetylcholine receptor), producing a rapid electrical response.
  • G-protein coupled receptors (GPCRs) - the largest receptor family, covered in depth in Chapter 2.
  • Enzyme-linked receptors - ligand binding activates an intrinsic or associated enzyme activity, most commonly a tyrosine kinase (e.g., receptor tyrosine kinases for growth factors, central to Chapter 5's discussion of oncogenes).

Second Messengers and Signal Amplification

Receptor activation typically triggers production of small, diffusible second messengers inside the cell (covered further in Chapter 2), which relay and amplify the signal - a single activated receptor can generate many second-messenger molecules, each of which can activate many downstream targets, producing a large response from a small initial signal.

Remark - Regulation of Signaling

Signaling pathways are just as tightly regulated as they are activated - through receptor desensitization and internalization, phosphatases that reverse kinase-driven activation, and negative-feedback loops - ensuring signals are transient and proportionate rather than runaway.
