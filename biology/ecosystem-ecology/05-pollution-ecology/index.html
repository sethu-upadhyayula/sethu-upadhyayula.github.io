5. Pollution Ecology

Pollution - the introduction of substances or energy into the environment at rates or concentrations exceeding an ecosystem's capacity to process them safely - affects ecosystems through several distinct mechanisms, covered in this chapter.

Nutrient Pollution and Eutrophication

Definition - Eutrophication

Excess nutrient input (typically nitrogen and phosphorus, Chapter 2, from agricultural runoff or wastewater) into aquatic ecosystems can trigger eutrophication - explosive algal growth that, upon algal death and decomposition, depletes dissolved oxygen and can create "dead zones" lethal to fish and other oxygen-dependent organisms, a direct ecological consequence of otherwise beneficial nutrients being supplied in ecologically damaging excess.

Toxic Pollutants and Bioaccumulation

Definition - Bioaccumulation and Biomagnification

Bioaccumulation is the buildup of a persistent, poorly excreted toxic substance within an individual organism over its lifetime. Biomagnification describes how this accumulated concentration increases at each successive trophic level (Chapter 1), since a predator consumes and concentrates the accumulated toxin burden of many prey individuals - meaning top predators, despite having no direct exposure advantage, often bear the highest toxic burden of any species in a food web.

Plastic Pollution

Plastic debris, particularly microplastics formed by the gradual fragmentation of larger plastic waste, has become a pervasive contaminant across virtually all ecosystems, including remote and deep-ocean environments, with ecological consequences (ingestion, physical entanglement, and potential vectoring of other pollutants) still being actively characterized.

Bioremediation as a Response

Microbial and plant-based approaches to actively removing or neutralizing pollutants from a contaminated environment - bioremediation and phytoremediation - are covered in detail in Environmental & Marine Biotechnology, offering biologically-based mitigation for several of the pollution categories introduced in this chapter.
