6. Sensory Photobiology and the Biological Clock

As sessile organisms, plants rely heavily on light not only as an energy source (Chapter 8 of Biochemistry) but as their primary source of environmental information - this chapter covers the photoreceptor systems that let a plant sense light quality, direction, and duration.

Plant Photoreceptors

The Three Major Photoreceptor Families
  • Phytochromes - sense red and far-red light, existing in two interconvertible forms that let the plant detect not just the presence of light but its spectral quality, useful for sensing shading by neighbouring plants.
  • Cryptochromes - sense blue and UV-A light, contributing to de-etiolation (the transition from dark-grown to light-grown seedling morphology) and entrainment of the circadian clock.
  • Phototropins - also blue-light receptors, primarily mediating phototropism (bending toward light) and light-driven stomatal opening.

Stomatal Movement

Stomata (Chapter 1) open and close through changes in the turgor pressure of their surrounding guard cells, driven by ion and water fluxes (Chapter 2 of Cell Biology) triggered by light (via phototropins), CO₂ concentration, and - under drought stress - abscisic acid signaling (Chapter 5), balancing the competing demands of CO₂ uptake for photosynthesis against water loss through transpiration (Chapter 3).

Photoperiodism

Definition - Photoperiodism

The ability to measure and respond to day length, most classically studied in the context of flowering time (Chapter 6 of Developmental Biology). Plants are broadly classified as short-day, long-day, or day-neutral depending on how flowering responds to day length, with the phytochrome system providing the underlying light-sensing mechanism.

The Circadian Clock

An internal circadian clock, built from interlocking transcription-translation feedback loops (Chapter 5 of Molecular Biology), allows plants to anticipate the daily light-dark cycle rather than merely react to it, synchronizing processes such as stomatal opening, photosynthetic gene expression, and hormone signaling to the appropriate time of day, and is itself entrained daily by photoreceptor input.
