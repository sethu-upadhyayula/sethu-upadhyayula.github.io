Plant Biology

Plant Biology covers the structure and physiology of vascular plants - how they are built, how they move water and nutrients over long distances without a pump, how a shared set of hormones coordinates growth throughout the plant body, and how plants sense and respond to their environment despite being unable to move away from it. Photosynthesis and respiration are covered in Chapter 8 of Biochemistry, and plant development and reproduction in Developmental Biology, so this subject focuses on anatomy, transport, hormones, and physiological responses.
