3. KVA and MVA

Two of the most significant post-crisis additions to the XVA suite are KVA, the cost of holding regulatory capital against a derivatives position over its lifetime, and MVA, the cost of funding the initial margin introduced in Chapter 2. Both adjustments are driven by regulation rather than by counterparty credit risk directly, and both have become first-order P&L and pricing considerations for any new long-dated trade.

This chapter develops KVA as the present value of the cost of capital a bank must hold against a trade under frameworks such as Basel III, and MVA as the present value of the funding spread paid on segregated initial margin balances. Together with CVA, DVA and FVA from Chapter 1, these adjustments complete the core XVA stack used in modern derivatives pricing.
