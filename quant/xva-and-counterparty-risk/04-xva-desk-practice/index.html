4. XVA Desk Practice

The preceding chapters developed each XVA component - CVA, DVA, FVA, the collateral mechanics behind them, and the capital and margin costs of KVA and MVA - largely at the level of a single trade or netting set. In practice, a bank's XVA desk operates at the portfolio level: it aggregates exposures across thousands of trades and hundreds of counterparties, computes a single set of valuation adjustments per netting set, and manages the resulting market and credit risk centrally.

This final chapter ties the XVA framework together from the perspective of an XVA desk: how netting sets define the unit of XVA computation and aggregation, how the desk hedges its CVA, FVA and other exposures in the market, and how the resulting hedging P&L is attributed and reported. This completes the picture of XVA as a living, actively-managed business within a derivatives dealer.
