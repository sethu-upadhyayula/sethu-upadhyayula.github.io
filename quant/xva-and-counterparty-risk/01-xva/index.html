1. CVA and XVA

The XVA framework - comprising CVA, DVA, FVA, KVA, MVA, and others - represents the most significant evolution in derivatives pricing since the financial crisis. XVA adjustments capture the real-world costs of trading OTC derivatives that classical risk-neutral pricing ignores: counterparty default risk, funding costs, capital requirements, and margin.

This chapter focuses on the three most important valuation adjustments: CVA (Credit Valuation Adjustment), the cost of counterparty default; DVA (Debit Valuation Adjustment), the benefit of one's own default; and FVA (Funding Valuation Adjustment), the cost of funding uncollateralised positions. These are central to the profit and loss of every derivatives desk at a major bank.
