2. Collateral and Margining

Chapter 1 derived CVA and FVA largely in terms of uncollateralised exposure profiles. In practice, the vast majority of OTC derivatives between major dealers are subject to a collateral agreement that posts variation margin daily, dramatically reducing - but never fully eliminating - counterparty exposure. Understanding how collateralisation works mechanically is essential to understanding why XVA charges are as small (or as large) as they are.

This chapter covers the Credit Support Annex (CSA) that governs variation margin exchange, and the post-crisis regime of mandatory initial margin for non-cleared derivatives under the ISDA Standard Initial Margin Model (SIMM). Initial margin introduces its own funding cost - previewed here and developed fully in Chapter 3 as the Margin Valuation Adjustment (MVA).
