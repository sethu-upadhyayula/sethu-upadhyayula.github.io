Equity Markets

A share of stock is a fractional ownership claim on a company's assets and future earnings; its price reflects the market's present-value assessment of those future cash flows. These notes lay the institutional and mathematical foundations of equity markets that underpin everything else in equity quantitative finance.

We examine the mechanics of how stocks are issued and traded, how dividends affect pricing, and how individual stocks are aggregated into indices that serve as benchmarks and tradeable assets in their own right. Understanding exchange rules, order types, corporate actions, and index construction is essential context for the derivative pricing theory developed in the other equity subjects.

Dividend treatment in particular is a recurring practical concern: discrete dividends create discontinuities in stock price dynamics that must be handled carefully in forward and options pricing — a theme that recurs throughout equity derivatives, Black-Scholes, and volatility products.
