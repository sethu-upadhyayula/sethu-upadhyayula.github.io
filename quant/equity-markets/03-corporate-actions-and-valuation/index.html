3. Corporate Actions and Equity Valuation

A stock's price does not move only in response to trading - it is mechanically adjusted by corporate events such as dividend payments, stock splits, and rights issues, each of which changes the relationship between price and per-share value without changing the firm's underlying worth. This chapter covers the mechanics of these corporate actions and the conventions used to adjust prices, share counts, and derivative contracts around them.

We then turn from the mechanics of price adjustment to the question of what a share is actually worth. Fundamental valuation approaches - the dividend discount model, discounted cash flow analysis, and relative valuation using multiples - provide the analytical toolkit for estimating intrinsic value and form the basis for the factor models and risk frameworks developed in the final chapter.
