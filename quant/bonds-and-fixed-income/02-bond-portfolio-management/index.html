2. Bond Portfolio Management

Chapter 1 developed the building blocks for pricing and risk-measuring a single bond: price, yield, duration, DV01, and convexity. A real fixed income book holds dozens or hundreds of bonds, and the portfolio manager's job is to combine those individual risk measures into a coherent strategy — matching the timing and sensitivity of assets to liabilities, and controlling how the portfolio behaves under both small and large interest rate moves.

This chapter covers duration matching and immunization, the classical technique for constructing a portfolio whose value is protected against parallel shifts in the yield curve, and then turns to convexity at the portfolio level — how the shape of a portfolio's cashflows (concentrated in a single bullet maturity versus spread across a barbell of short and long bonds) affects its performance when rates move by more than a few basis points.
