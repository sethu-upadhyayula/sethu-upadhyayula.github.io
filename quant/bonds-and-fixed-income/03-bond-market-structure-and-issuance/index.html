3. Bond Market Structure and Issuance

So far we have treated bond prices and yields as given market data. This chapter steps back to ask where those prices come from: how new bonds enter the market through the primary market, and how they are subsequently bought and sold in the secondary market. Understanding this plumbing matters because the mechanics of issuance and trading directly affect liquidity, pricing, and the financing costs that show up in every relative value calculation.

We first cover primary markets — the auction process by which governments issue new debt, and the syndication process used by corporate issuers — and then turn to secondary market trading structure and the repo market, the mechanism by which bonds are financed and through which "specialness" in individual securities is expressed and arbitraged.
