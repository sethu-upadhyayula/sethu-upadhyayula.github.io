4. Crises, Contagion, and Case Studies

The preceding chapters described EM FX regimes, central bank policy responses, and the carry and volatility risk premia that investors are compensated for bearing — much of which is framed around the possibility of a sharp, discontinuous currency depreciation. This chapter turns directly to that event: the currency crisis itself. We develop the classic theoretical models of how fixed and managed exchange rate regimes break down under speculative pressure, from mechanical reserve-depletion dynamics to self-fulfilling runs driven purely by shifts in expectations.

The second section grounds this theory in three of the most-studied episodes in modern EM FX history — the 1997 Asian Financial Crisis, Argentina's currency board collapse in 2001 and its subsequent 2018 crisis, and the recurring Turkish lira crises — drawing out the common threads (overvalued pegs, current account and external financing imbalances, loss of reserves and credibility, and contagion across countries with similar vulnerabilities) that link the abstract models of Section 4.1 to real-world outcomes.
