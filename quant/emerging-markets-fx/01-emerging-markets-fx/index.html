1. EM Currency Market Structure

Emerging market currency markets are structured differently from G10 FX in ways that matter for pricing, hedging, and risk management. Many EM currencies are subject to capital controls that restrict who can hold the currency, when it can be converted, and how much can be moved across borders. To allow international investors to take exposure despite these restrictions, an offshore market in non-deliverable forwards (NDFs) has developed for most major EM currencies - cash-settled contracts referencing a published fixing rate, traded entirely outside the onshore banking system.

This chapter develops the mechanics of NDFs and the offshore markets that trade them, then examines what happens when an onshore deliverable market and an offshore NDF market coexist for the same currency: the resulting onshore-offshore basis, the channels through which capital controls drive that basis, and the fixing risk that arises because NDF settlement depends on a rate published by an authority that may itself be a party to the policy debate the market is trying to price.
