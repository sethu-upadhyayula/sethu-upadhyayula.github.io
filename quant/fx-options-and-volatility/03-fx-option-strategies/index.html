3. FX Option Strategies

Having established how vanilla FX options are priced, quoted, and how their volatility surface is built and behaves, this chapter turns to how these options are combined into trading strategies. Vanilla options are rarely traded in isolation by sophisticated participants — instead, combinations such as straddles, strangles, risk reversals, and collars are used to express precise views on direction, volatility, and the shape of the smile, often while being structured to have zero or near-zero upfront cost.

The first section covers these vanilla combinations — their payoff profiles, typical use cases, and a worked zero-cost collar pricing example. The second section turns to volatility trading proper: how a delta-hedged option position is used to trade realized volatility against implied volatility, the mechanics of gamma scalping in FX, and how calendar spreads across tenors express views on the term structure of volatility.
