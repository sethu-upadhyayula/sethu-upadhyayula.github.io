2. FX Volatility Surface Construction

Chapter 1 showed how the FX volatility surface is anchored by three market-quoted pillars at each tenor — the ATM volatility, the risk reversal, and the butterfly — and how these translate into implied volatilities at the 25-delta call and put strikes. In practice, however, a pricing or risk system needs implied volatility at any strike (or delta) and tenor, not just the quoted pillars. This chapter covers the interpolation and parameterisation methods used to build a continuous, arbitrage-free volatility surface from a sparse set of market quotes.

We begin with smile interpolation methods — the vanna-volga approach that is ubiquitous in FX desks, and SABR-style parametric fitting — and how interpolation is performed across both the delta and strike dimensions of the smile. We then turn to volatility surface dynamics: how the surface is assumed to move as spot moves (sticky strike, sticky delta, sticky moneyness), and why this assumption is not just a modelling nicety but directly determines the P&L of a vega-hedged options book.
