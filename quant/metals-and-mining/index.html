Metals and Mining

This subject covers the precious, base, battery, and bulk metals markets - gold, silver, copper, aluminum, lithium, nickel, iron ore, and steel - including their pricing conventions, the exchanges and benchmarks that set global prices, and the futures, forwards, and options markets used to hedge and speculate on metal prices. We build from the monetary role of gold and silver and the cost-of-carry framework for base metals, through the operational mechanics of the London Metal Exchange (LME) and the carry trades built on its curve, to mining economics and producer hedging, and finally to the battery metals and bulk commodity markets that underpin the energy transition and steel production.

The first chapter lays the foundations: gold and silver as monetary metals with deep lease markets, and the contrasting industrial demand drivers of copper and aluminum. The second chapter goes inside the LME's distinctive contract structure, warehousing system, and the cash-3M spread trades that define metals curve trading. The third chapter turns to the supply side - mining cost curves, all-in sustaining costs, and the forward sales and option structures producers use to hedge revenue. The fourth chapter covers battery metals (lithium, nickel, cobalt) whose pricing is still maturing relative to exchange-traded base metals, and the bulk commodities - iron ore and steel - whose pricing is dominated by index benchmarks and freight economics.
