1. Crude Oil Markets and Refining Economics

Crude oil is the largest commodity market by traded value and the reference point for the rest of the energy complex. This chapter develops the structure of the global crude market: the WTI and Brent benchmarks that anchor pricing, the physical attributes (API gravity, sulphur content) that determine how individual crude grades trade relative to those benchmarks, and the mechanics of the Cushing, Oklahoma delivery hub that underlies the WTI futures contract.

The second section turns downstream, to the refining economics that connect crude prices to the prices of the products — gasoline and distillates — that refiners produce. We cover crack spreads as a measure of refining profitability, the seasonal product markets that drive much of the term structure in refined products, the supply-side role of OPEC+ in setting the overall level of crude prices, and the storage economics that link physical inventory levels to the shape of the futures curve.
