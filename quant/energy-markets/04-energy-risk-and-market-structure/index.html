4. Energy Risk and Market Structure

The final chapter steps back from pricing mechanics to the structural and political forces that shape energy markets at the highest level. We examine the role of OPEC+ in managing global crude supply through production quotas, the question of member compliance with those quotas, and how geopolitical events — wars, sanctions, and supply disruptions — transmit into prices, including the role of strategic petroleum reserves as a shock absorber.

The second section looks forward to the energy transition and the markets that have emerged around it: carbon pricing through cap-and-trade systems such as the EU Emissions Trading System, renewable energy certificates that monetise the environmental attributes of clean generation, and the transition risk that decarbonisation poses to the value of fossil-fuel forward curves and assets. We close with the basic mechanics of carbon credit derivatives.
