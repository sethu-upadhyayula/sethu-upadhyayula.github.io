4.2 Index Pricing

A CDS index trades with a standardised fixed coupon (e.g. 100 bps for investment grade). Because the fair (par) index spread generally differs from this fixed coupon, an upfront payment is exchanged at trade inception. The central pricing problem is to convert between the quoted index spread and the upfront payment, and to compute the index RPV01 for risk management.

Index as a Portfolio of Single-Name CDS

Definition — Index NPV

A CDS index on $n$ equally-weighted names can be valued as the equally-weighted portfolio of the constituent single-name CDS. For the protection buyer, the NPV of the index at current market spreads is: $$\text{NPV}_{\text{idx}} = \frac{1}{n}\sum_{i=1}^{n} \text{NPV}_i$$ where $\text{NPV}_i = (s_i^* - c) \times N_i \times \text{RPV01}_i$ is the MTM of the $i$-th constituent CDS (with fixed coupon $c$ and notional $N_i = N/n$). However, the index is generally priced using a single flat hazard rate calibrated to the quoted index spread, rather than the full constituent curve.

Flat Curve Pricing — Upfront and Running

Definition — Upfront Payment

Given the quoted index spread $s_{\text{idx}}$ and the standard fixed coupon $c$, the upfront payment $U$ (paid by the protection buyer if $s_{\text{idx}} > c$, received if $s_{\text{idx}} < c$) is: $$U = (s_{\text{idx}} - c) \times N \times \text{RPV01}_{\text{idx}}$$ where $\text{RPV01}_{\text{idx}}$ is the index risky annuity, computed by bootstrapping a flat hazard rate from the index spread: $$s_{\text{idx}} \approx \frac{(1-R)\,\lambda}{1} \implies \lambda = \frac{s_{\text{idx}}}{1-R}$$ then: $$\text{RPV01}_{\text{idx}} = \sum_{i=1}^{n_c} \delta_i \cdot e^{-r(t_i)\,t_i} \cdot e^{-\lambda\,t_i}$$ summing over all quarterly coupon dates $\{t_i\}$ to maturity.

Example — CDX.NA.IG Upfront Calculation

CDX.NA.IG S43 5Y trades at a quoted spread of 72 bps, fixed coupon $c = 100$ bps. Risk-free rate = 4.5%, recovery = 40%, notional = \$10m.

Implied hazard rate: $\lambda = 0.0072 / 0.60 = 1.20\%$ p.a.

Approximate RPV01 for 5Y quarterly: $\text{RPV01} \approx \sum_{i=1}^{20} 0.25 \cdot e^{-(0.045+0.012) \times 0.25i} \approx 4.30$ years.

Upfront: $U = (0.0072 - 0.0100) \times \$10\text{m} \times 4.30 = -0.0028 \times \$43\text{m} = -\$120,400$.

The protection buyer receives \$120,400 upfront because the index spread (72 bps) is below the fixed coupon (100 bps) — the index is cheap relative to the coupon level, so the seller compensates the buyer at inception.

Index DV01 and CS01

Definition — Index CS01

The index CS01 is the change in MTM for a 1 bp widening of the index spread: $$\text{CS01}_{\text{idx}} \approx N \times \text{RPV01}_{\text{idx}} \times 0.0001$$ For a \$10m 5Y IG index position with RPV01 $\approx 4.30$: CS01 $\approx \$10\text{m} \times 4.30 \times 0.0001 = \$4,300$ per bp. A 10 bp spread widening increases the protection buyer's MTM by approximately \$43,000.

Roll Mechanics

Remark — Index Roll Trade

At each semi-annual roll date, investors wishing to maintain index exposure must sell the old (off-the-run) series and buy the new (on-the-run) series — the roll trade. The roll spread is the difference in upfront payments between the two series, adjusted for any change in composition and fixed coupon. The roll is a significant source of flow on credit desks and creates basis between OTR and OFR series. Dealers make markets in the roll itself as a single package trade.
