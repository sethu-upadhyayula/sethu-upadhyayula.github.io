3.3 CDS Pricing

CDS pricing decomposes the contract into two legs: the premium leg (periodic payments by the protection buyer) and the protection leg (contingent payment by the protection seller on credit event). At inception, the fair (par) CDS spread equates the present values of the two legs. After inception, the mark-to-market (MTM) value reflects changes in the credit curve.

Premium Leg

Definition — Premium Leg PV

The premium leg pays spread $s$ on notional $N$ at coupon dates $\{t_1, t_2, \ldots, t_n\}$ with period lengths $\{\delta_i\}$ (in years), conditional on survival. Its present value (from the protection buyer's perspective, a liability) is: $$\text{PV}_{\text{prem}} = s \cdot N \sum_{i=1}^{n} \delta_i \cdot P(0, t_i) \cdot Q(t_i)$$ An accrual term accounts for the partial coupon accrued up to a mid-period default. Approximating the default as occurring at the midpoint of each period: $$\text{PV}_{\text{prem}} \approx s \cdot N \sum_{i=1}^{n} \delta_i \cdot P(0, t_i) \cdot Q(t_i) + s \cdot N \sum_{i=1}^{n} \frac{\delta_i}{2} \cdot P(0, t_i) \cdot [Q(t_{i-1}) - Q(t_i)]$$

Protection Leg

Definition — Protection Leg PV

The protection leg pays $(1-R)\cdot N$ at the default time $\tau$ if default occurs before maturity $T$. Its present value (an asset to the protection buyer) is: $$\text{PV}_{\text{prot}} = (1-R) \cdot N \int_0^T P(0,t)\,(-dQ(t))= (1-R) \cdot N \int_0^T P(0,t)\,\lambda(t)\,Q(t)\,dt$$ Numerically, discretising over small time steps $\Delta t_i$: $$\text{PV}_{\text{prot}} \approx (1-R) \cdot N \sum_{i} P(0, \bar{t}_i)\,[Q(t_{i-1}) - Q(t_i)]$$ where $\bar{t}_i$ is the midpoint of interval $(t_{i-1}, t_i]$.

Par Spread and RPV01

Theorem — Par CDS Spread

The fair (par) CDS spread is the spread that makes the NPV of the contract zero at inception: $$\text{NPV} = \text{PV}_{\text{prot}} - \text{PV}_{\text{prem}} = 0$$ Solving for $s$: $$s^* = \frac{\text{PV}_{\text{prot}}}{N \cdot \text{RPV01}}$$ where the Risky PV01 (RPV01) or duration of the CDS is: $$\text{RPV01} = \sum_{i=1}^{n} \delta_i \cdot P(0, t_i) \cdot Q(t_i)$$ RPV01 measures the change in PV per 1 bp change in the running spread and is the CDS equivalent of duration for bonds.

Mark-to-Market and Upfront Payment

Definition — CDS Mark-to-Market

For a CDS entered at running spread $s_0$ and now marked at current par spread $s^*$, the MTM value to the protection buyer is: $$\text{MTM} = (s^* - s_0) \cdot N \cdot \text{RPV01}$$ Under post-2009 SNAC conventions, CDS trade with a fixed coupon $c$ (e.g. 100 bps or 500 bps). The upfront payment $U$ exchanged at trade inception is: $$U = (s^* - c) \cdot N \cdot \text{RPV01}$$ If $s^* > c$, the protection buyer pays upfront; if $s^* < c$, the protection seller pays upfront. The upfront is quoted as a percentage of notional.

Example — CDS Pricing

A 5-year CDS on a reference entity with flat hazard rate $\lambda = 3.33\%$, risk-free rate $r = 3\%$, recovery $R = 40\%$. Quarterly coupons, $\delta_i = 0.25$.

RPV01 $\approx \sum_{i=1}^{20} 0.25 \cdot e^{-0.03 \cdot 0.25i} \cdot e^{-0.0333 \cdot 0.25i} \approx 0.25 \sum_{i=1}^{20} e^{-0.0633 \cdot 0.25i} \approx 4.12$ years.

Protection leg: $(1-0.4) \times \int_0^5 e^{-(0.03+0.0333)t} \times 0.0333\,dt \approx 0.6 \times 0.0333 \times \frac{1-e^{-0.0633 \times 5}}{0.0633} \approx 0.1233$.

Par spread: $s^* = 0.1233 / 4.12 \approx 199$ bps — consistent with the approximation $s \approx \lambda(1-R) = 3.33\% \times 0.6 = 200$ bps.

Remark — CS01 and Credit Sensitivity

The CS01 (Credit Spread 01) of a CDS position is the change in MTM for a 1 bp parallel shift in the CDS spread curve: CS01 $\approx N \times \text{RPV01} \times 0.0001$. For a protection buyer, CS01 is positive (spread widening increases MTM). CS01 is the credit analogue of DV01 and is the primary risk measure for CDS portfolios.
