2. Loans

Loans are the most fundamental credit instrument: a lender advances capital to a borrower, who repays principal and interest over time. Unlike bonds, loans are bilateral, typically floating-rate, and often carry covenants protecting the lender. This chapter covers the mechanics of loan structuring and pricing, the modelling of voluntary prepayments, and the quantitative treatment of default risk.

Loan markets are divided into investment grade and leveraged (high-yield) segments. Leveraged loans — originated by banks and syndicated to institutional investors including CLO managers — underpin the structured credit market studied in Chapter 5.
