2. FX Accumulators and Decumulators

Accumulators and decumulators are structured products built from a strip of forward-like fixings, similar in spirit to the target redemption forwards of the previous chapter but typically without a target-based knockout - instead, a knock-out (or knock-in) is usually triggered by spot trading through a barrier level on any individual fixing date. They are among the most commonly traded structured FX products sold to corporates, asset managers, and private banking clients, generally marketed as a way to accumulate (or sell down) a foreign currency position at a more favourable average rate than a single forward.

The first section of this chapter sets out the payoff mechanics of accumulators and decumulators, the role of the knockout barrier, and why corporates find them attractive relative to a plain forward or vanilla option strip. The second section turns to the risk management challenges these structures pose for the dealer who sells them - in particular the path-dependent Greeks that build up over the life of the structure and the gap risk that crystallises if spot jumps through the knockout barrier between fixings.
