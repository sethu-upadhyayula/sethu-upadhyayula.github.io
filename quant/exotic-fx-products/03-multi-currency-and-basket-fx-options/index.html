3. Multi-Currency and Basket FX Options

The first two chapters dealt with exotics on a single currency pair, where the only stochastic driver is the spot rate of that pair. This chapter introduces exotic FX products whose payoff depends on two or more underlying rates simultaneously — either multiple currency pairs, or a single currency pair combined with the exchange rate used to convert the payoff back into the investor's domestic currency. The new ingredient these structures bring is correlation: the joint distribution of the underlying rates, not just their individual marginal distributions, drives the price.

The first section covers basket options, whose payoff depends on a weighted combination of several exchange rates, and quanto options, whose foreign-currency payoff is converted into the investor's domestic currency at a pre-fixed rate, introducing a correlation-dependent drift adjustment known as the quanto adjustment. The second section covers best-of and worst-of options, whose payoff depends on the maximum or minimum of several underlyings' performance, and discusses how their value depends acutely on the correlation between those underlyings, including the practical difficulties of calibrating that correlation from the market.
