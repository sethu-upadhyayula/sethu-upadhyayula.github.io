4. Rate Option Risk Management

The final chapter turns from pricing models to the day-to-day risk management of an interest rate options book. Every cap, floor, swaption, and exotic structure introduced in the previous chapters carries sensitivities to the underlying forward rate or swap rate, to the discount curve used to compute annuities and discount factors, and to the implied volatilities used to price it. Understanding and decomposing these sensitivities — the Greeks — is what allows a desk to hedge a book of options against small moves in rates and volatility rather than re-pricing from scratch after every market move.

Beyond single-trade Greeks, a real book's volatility risk lives on an entire surface (or cube) of calibrated parameters spanning expiries, tenors, and strikes. This chapter covers how that surface is calibrated — typically via SABR, as introduced in Section 1.3 — and how vega risk is bucketed across the surface so that a trader can see not just an aggregate vega number but where on the expiry/tenor grid that risk is concentrated. It closes with a discussion of model risk: the risk that the calibrated model itself, however well it fits today's prices, misprices the future evolution of the surface or the hedging instruments used to offset it.
