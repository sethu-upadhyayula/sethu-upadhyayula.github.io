3. Bermudan and Exotic Rate Options

European caps, floors, and swaptions can be priced off a single forward rate or swap rate using the Black or normal models of Chapter 2. Many traded instruments, however, embed optionality that depends on the joint distribution of rates across multiple future dates, or on more than one underlying rate at once — and these cannot be reduced to a single Black formula. This chapter introduces the two most important such instruments: Bermudan swaptions, which embed a sequence of exercise decisions, and CMS-linked products, whose payoffs depend on swap rates observed under a measure that is not their natural one.

Pricing Bermudan swaptions requires a full term-structure model — a model that describes the joint dynamics of the entire forward curve, such as the LIBOR Market Model (LMM) or the Heath-Jarrow-Morton (HJM) framework — together with a numerical method capable of handling the embedded optimal-stopping problem. CMS products, by contrast, can often be handled with a static replication argument layered on top of the existing swaption smile, via the CMS convexity adjustment. Both topics illustrate how the simple building blocks of Chapters 1 and 2 combine into the exotic structures that dominate much of the flow in interest rate derivatives desks.
