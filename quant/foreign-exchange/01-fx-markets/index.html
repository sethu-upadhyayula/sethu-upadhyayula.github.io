1. FX Markets

This chapter lays the groundwork for quantitative FX analysis by establishing the mechanics of the spot market. We define exchange rates precisely, explain the base/quote currency convention that is the source of much confusion for newcomers, and examine the microstructure of the interbank FX market — the largest decentralised financial market in the world.

A precise understanding of quoting conventions is not merely pedantic: it is operationally essential. Whether a delta hedge should buy or sell the base currency, how a P&L attribution is computed, and how Greeks transform under currency inversion all depend on getting the conventions exactly right. These fundamentals carry forward into every subsequent chapter.
