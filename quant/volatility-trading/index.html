Volatility Trading

Volatility is not merely a parameter in an option pricing formula - it is an asset class in its own right, with its own instruments, market participants, and trading strategies. These notes examine the products that allow market participants to take direct views on, or hedge exposure to, the volatility of an underlying asset, separately from its price direction.

We begin with the implied volatility surface, the market's representation of option prices across strikes and maturities, and the dynamics that govern how it evolves - including the local and stochastic volatility models used to describe and trade it. We then turn to variance swaps, which provide a model-free way to trade realized variance, and the replication arguments that underpin them.

Finally, we examine the VIX index and related volatility benchmarks, which translate the implied volatility surface into a single tradeable "fear gauge" with its own futures and options market - together with the volatility risk premium and the structural risks that arise when large amounts of capital are positioned short volatility.
