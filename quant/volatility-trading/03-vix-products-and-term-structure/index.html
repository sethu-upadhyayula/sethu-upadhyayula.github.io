3. VIX Products and Term Structure

The VIX index, introduced in Section 1.3 as the market's model-free measure of 30-day implied volatility on the S&P 500, is not directly tradeable — but a large and liquid market of futures, options, and exchange-traded products has been built around it. This chapter takes a closer look at how the VIX is constructed conceptually, and at the term structure of VIX futures, which is one of the most closely watched curves in volatility markets.

We then examine VIX options and the family of volatility exchange-traded products that aim to give investors exposure to VIX futures, focusing in particular on the structural decay these products experience when the VIX futures curve is in contango — a feature that has caused dramatic losses for long-term holders of short-volatility ETPs.
