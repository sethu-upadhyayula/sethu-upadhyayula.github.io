4. Systematic Volatility Strategies

The preceding chapters established that volatility, like a traded asset, has its own risk premia, term structure, and exchange-traded wrappers. This final chapter turns to how these building blocks are assembled into systematic strategies - programmes that take a structural, repeatable view on volatility rather than a one-off directional bet, and that are run by dedicated volatility funds as well as by overlay programmes within broader portfolios.

The two themes covered are, in a sense, two sides of the same coin. The first is the systematic harvesting of the volatility risk premium - the empirical tendency for implied volatility to exceed subsequently realized volatility - through short option positions. The second is tail risk hedging, which uses long convex positions (often in VIX derivatives, as discussed in Chapter 3) to protect a portfolio against the very tail events that short-volatility strategies are most exposed to. Understanding both sides, and how they interact, is essential to running either responsibly.
