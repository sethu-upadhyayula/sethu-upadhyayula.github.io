2. Yield Curve Construction

Discount factors, spot rates, and forward rates are not directly observable at every maturity — the market quotes prices for a finite set of liquid instruments (deposits, futures, FRAs, and swaps), each with its own maturity and conventions. Yield curve construction is the process of inverting these market quotes into a continuous discount curve $T \mapsto P(0,T)$ that exactly reprices the input instruments and provides discount factors and forward rates at any maturity needed for valuation.

This chapter covers bootstrapping — the sequential algorithm for solving the discount curve from market instruments ordered by maturity — and interpolation, which fills in the curve between the discrete maturities ("pillars") implied by those instruments. Together these techniques underpin every pricing and risk system in the rates business, and the choices made here (which instruments, which interpolation scheme, single- vs. multi-curve) have material, sometimes surprising, effects on hedging and valuation.
