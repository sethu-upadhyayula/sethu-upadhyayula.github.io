4. Hedging, Regulation, and Market Structure

This final chapter brings together the pricing and risk concepts from earlier chapters into the practical strategies used by commercial participants — farmers, grain elevators, and food and feed processors — to manage price risk through the production and merchandising cycle. We cover hedge ratio determination, the use of futures and options in combination, and worked examples of complete hedging programs from the perspective of both producers and end users.

The second section steps back to consider the broader market structure within which this hedging activity takes place: position limits imposed by the Commodity Futures Trading Commission (CFTC) to prevent excessive speculation, the role of commodity index investment and its associated roll yield, exchange-for-physical (EFP) transactions that bridge futures and cash markets, and the overall taxonomy of participants — commercials, managed money, and index funds — whose interaction determines market liquidity and price formation.
