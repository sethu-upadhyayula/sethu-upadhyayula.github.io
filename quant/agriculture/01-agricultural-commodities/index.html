1. Agricultural Commodity Fundamentals

This chapter establishes the institutional and economic foundations of agricultural commodity markets. We begin with the major row crops - corn, wheat, and soybeans - and the oilseed complex, covering contract specifications and the calendar of USDA reports (WASDE, Acreage and Planting Intentions, Crop Progress) that drive scheduled volatility in these markets. Understanding what each report measures, when it is released, and how the market positions ahead of it is essential to trading or hedging in grain markets.

The second section ties these institutional features to the pricing framework: the seasonal planting-growing-harvest cycle that shapes the shape of agricultural futures curves, the storage cost and convenience yield model that explains carry and inversions within a crop year, the outsized role of weather as a supply shock, and the distinct characteristics of soft commodities - coffee, sugar, and cotton - whose tropical growing regions and cross-linkages to energy markets (via ethanol) set them apart from temperate-zone grains. Together these two sections provide the indispensable foundation for the futures, basis, options, and hedging material that follows.
