3. Curve Risk and Sensitivities

Once a discount curve has been built (Chapter 2), the next question for any trading desk is: how does the value of a position change when the curve moves? Every rates portfolio is exposed to changes in the level, slope, and curvature of the yield curve, and quantifying that exposure is the basis of both daily risk management and hedging.

This chapter develops the standard sensitivity measures used in practice - DV01 (dollar value of a basis point), PV01, and key rate durations - and shows how they are aggregated into a risk ladder and used to construct hedges. These measures translate an abstract discount curve into a small number of numbers that traders, risk managers, and regulators can act on directly.
