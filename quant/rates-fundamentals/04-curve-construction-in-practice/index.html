4. Curve Construction in Practice

Chapter 2 introduced bootstrapping and interpolation as a sequential, instrument-by-instrument procedure. In production curve-building systems, the picture is more involved: curves are calibrated jointly across many interdependent instruments and curves, and must reproduce market features that a textbook bootstrap glosses over.

This chapter covers the global calibration algorithms used to solve for an entire curve (or set of curves) simultaneously, and the turn-of-year and seasonality effects that cause real money market and OIS curves to deviate from the smooth shapes assumed so far. These practical issues matter directly for daily P&L: a curve that reprices its inputs to the penny but ignores a known year-end funding spike will misvalue any trade with cash flows over that date.
