2. FX Funding and the Basis

Chapter 1 established covered interest rate parity (CIP) as the no-arbitrage relationship pinning down forward rates, and noted in passing that a persistent, non-zero cross-currency basis has existed since the 2008 financial crisis. This chapter examines that basis in depth: why CIP held so tightly before the crisis, what changed afterward, and how the basis is best understood as a price for funding and balance sheet rather than a pure arbitrage opportunity.

The first section works through the CIP formula and its breakdown, including a worked example of computing a CIP deviation directly from market rates. The second section connects the basis to its underlying drivers — funding cost differentials across currencies, the role of collateral and CSA currency choice in derivatives valuation, and the money market spreads that ultimately determine how wide the basis trades. Together, these sections give the funding-side intuition needed before moving on to forward curve construction in Chapter 3.
