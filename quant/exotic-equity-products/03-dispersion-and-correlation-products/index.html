3. Dispersion and Correlation Products

Chapter 2 showed that correlation between underlyings is a first-order pricing input for basket and rainbow options. This chapter turns correlation itself into the primary traded object. Dispersion trades take a view on the relationship between an index's implied volatility and the implied volatilities of its individual constituents — a relationship governed almost entirely by implied correlation. Correlation swaps and related products go further, packaging realized or implied correlation directly into a tradeable payoff.

Section 3.1 develops the classic dispersion trade: selling index volatility while buying single-stock volatility on the index's constituents, and how the P&L of this position decomposes into a bet on realized correlation. Section 3.2 covers correlation swaps directly — their payoff structure, the distinction between realized and implied correlation, and the correlation skew that arises because implied correlation, like implied volatility, varies systematically with the strike or moneyness of the basket option used to back it out.
