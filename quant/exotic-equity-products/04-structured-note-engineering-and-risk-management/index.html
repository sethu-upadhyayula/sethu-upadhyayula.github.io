4. Structured Note Engineering and Risk Management

The preceding chapters introduced the building blocks — barrier and digital options, autocallables, path-dependent and multi-asset payoffs, and dispersion/correlation products. This final chapter brings these pieces together from the perspective of an issuer constructing and risk-managing a structured note business: how a structured note is decomposed into a bond component and an option component for pricing and margin purposes, and how an issuer manages the aggregate risk of a book containing many exotic positions.

Section 4.1 works through the bond-plus-option decomposition that underlies essentially every structured note, including how an issuer's margin is embedded in the pricing, with a full worked example of decomposing a representative note. Section 4.2 turns to the risk management of an exotic equity book as a whole: the Greeks that matter most for barrier and path-dependent positions (including the gap and pin risk concentrated near knock levels and observation dates), the hedging challenges that arise because exotic payoffs cannot always be perfectly replicated with liquid instruments, and the model risk inherent in marking and hedging a book of products whose value depends on assumptions — correlation, skew dynamics, discrete monitoring — that cannot be directly observed in the market.
