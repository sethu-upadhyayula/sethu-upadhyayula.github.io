2. Path-Dependent and Multi-Asset Options

Many of the most heavily traded exotic equity derivatives derive their value not just from where the underlying ends up, but from the path it takes to get there, or from the joint behavior of several underlyings together. Asian and lookback options replace the single terminal price in a vanilla payoff with an average or an extremum observed over the option's life, fundamentally changing both the option's value and its volatility exposure. Basket and rainbow options extend the payoff to multiple underlyings, introducing correlation as a first-class pricing input alongside volatility.

This chapter develops the intuition and pricing mechanics for both families. Section 2.1 covers Asian and lookback options, including why averaging reduces an Asian option's effective volatility relative to a vanilla option on the same underlying, and how lookback payoffs on the running maximum or minimum behave. Section 2.2 turns to basket options - payoffs on a weighted sum of several underlyings - and rainbow options, whose payoffs depend on the best- or worst-performing asset in a group, with a particular focus on how correlation between the constituents drives the option's value.
