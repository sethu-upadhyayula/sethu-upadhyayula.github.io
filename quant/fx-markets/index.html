FX Markets

The foreign exchange spot market is the world's largest and most liquid financial market, with daily turnover exceeding $7 trillion. These notes lay the groundwork for quantitative FX analysis by establishing the mechanics of the spot market: how exchange rates are defined and quoted, the base/quote currency convention, the structure of bid-offer spreads and pips, and the tiered, decentralised microstructure of the interbank FX market.

A precise understanding of quoting conventions is not merely pedantic: it is operationally essential. Whether a delta hedge should buy or sell the base currency, how a P&L attribution is computed, and how Greeks transform under currency inversion all depend on getting the conventions exactly right. The second chapter examines the structure and liquidity of the FX market itself — its participants, venues, and settlement infrastructure — while the third turns to order flow and microstructure, and the fourth to the macroeconomic drivers that anchor exchange rates over longer horizons. These fundamentals carry forward into every other area of FX — forwards and swaps, options and volatility, and exotic products.
