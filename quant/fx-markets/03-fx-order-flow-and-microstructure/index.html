3. FX Order Flow and Microstructure

Having established who trades FX and through which venues (Chapter 2), this chapter examines how trading actually happens at the level of individual orders. We survey the menu of order types available to FX participants — from simple market and limit orders to algorithmic execution strategies such as TWAP and VWAP — and discuss how transaction cost analysis (TCA) is used to measure and manage the cost of execution.

We then turn to the deeper question of how prices in FX are formed. Order flow is not merely a record of completed trades; it carries information about participants' views and intentions, and dealers must continuously infer how much of the flow they see is "informed" versus "uninformed." We examine the concept of order flow toxicity, the central role dealers play in aggregating and incorporating information into prices, and the adverse selection problem that arises whenever a market maker quotes a price without knowing the informational content of the order that will arrive against it.
