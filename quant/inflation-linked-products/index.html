Inflation-Linked Products

Inflation-linked products span a spectrum from cash bonds to derivatives to portfolio-level hedging strategies, all built around a common set of objects: the price index used for indexation, the real yield curve, and the breakeven inflation rate that links real and nominal markets. These notes develop that framework from the ground up, starting with the mechanics of inflation-linked bonds such as U.S. TIPS and UK index-linked gilts, and building toward the derivatives, risk-premium decompositions, and portfolio applications used by practitioners.

The first chapter covers inflation-linked bond fundamentals - index ratio mechanics, real yield curve construction, breakeven inflation, and the Fisher equation that relates nominal, real, and inflation rates. The second chapter turns to inflation derivatives: zero-coupon and year-on-year inflation swaps, and inflation caps, floors, and options, the instruments used to trade and hedge inflation exposures directly without holding physical bonds.

The third chapter examines what breakeven inflation actually represents - decomposing it into expected inflation, an inflation risk premium, and a liquidity premium, and addressing the seasonality and indexation-lag effects that complicate real-world inflation curve construction. The fourth chapter applies this machinery to portfolio problems: hedging inflation-linked liabilities for pension funds and insurers, and identifying relative value across the breakeven and real yield curves. Together these four chapters provide a complete working framework for analysing and trading inflation-linked products.
