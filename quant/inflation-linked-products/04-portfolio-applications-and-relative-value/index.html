4. Portfolio Applications and Relative Value

The preceding chapters built up the instruments — inflation-linked bonds and inflation derivatives — and the analytical framework — breakeven decomposition, seasonality, and indexation lag — needed to understand inflation-linked markets. This final chapter applies that framework to two practical problems that dominate how these instruments are actually used: hedging inflation-linked liabilities, and identifying relative value across the inflation curve.

The first section addresses liability-driven investment (LDI), where pension funds and insurers with inflation-linked liabilities use linkers and inflation swaps to construct hedges, focusing on how hedge ratios are derived from liability cash flow profiles and the assets available to hedge them. The second section turns to relative value, covering breakeven curve trades such as steepeners and flatteners, real yield curve positioning, and how breakevens can be compared across markets — for example U.S. TIPS versus UK index-linked gilts — to identify mispricings, illustrated with a worked trade example.
