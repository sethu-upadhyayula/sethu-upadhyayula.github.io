1. Inflation-Linked Bond Fundamentals

Inflation-linked bonds (ILBs) - such as U.S. Treasury Inflation-Protected Securities (TIPS) and UK Index-Linked Gilts - pay coupons and principal that are adjusted for changes in a reference price index, typically a national CPI, protecting investors from the erosion of purchasing power that affects fixed-coupon nominal bonds. This chapter develops the indexation mechanics that link a bond's cash flows to the price index, the construction of the real yield curve that such bonds trade on, and the breakeven inflation framework that connects real yields to their nominal counterparts.

The first section works through index ratio mechanics in detail - the CPI reference lag, daily interpolation between monthly index releases, and the resulting formulas for indexed principal and coupon cash flows - and shows how these cash flows are discounted on a real yield curve. The second section develops breakeven inflation as the wedge between nominal and real yields, introduces the Fisher equation in both its approximate and exact forms, and examines why market-implied breakevens are not a pure measure of expected inflation. Together these two sections provide the essential vocabulary - index ratios, real yields, and breakevens - used throughout the remaining chapters on inflation derivatives, risk premia, and portfolio applications.
