2.1 Index Composition

CDS indices are constructed from equally-weighted baskets of single-name CDS, selected and rolled according to rules set by the index administrator (IHS Markit, now part of S&P Global). The two main families are CDX (covering North American and emerging market entities) and iTraxx (covering European and Asian-ex-Japan entities).

Major Index Families

Definition — CDX and iTraxx Sub-Indices
  • CDX.NA.IG — 125 North American investment-grade entities, equally weighted at $1/125$ of notional each.
  • CDX.NA.HY — 100 North American high-yield entities.
  • iTraxx Europe (Main) — 125 European investment-grade entities.
  • iTraxx Crossover (XO) — 75 sub-investment-grade European entities.

Series and Roll Dates

Each index family issues a new "series" semi-annually, on 20 March and 20 September, coinciding with the standard CDS IMM dates. At each roll:

  • Constituents that no longer meet eligibility criteria (e.g., credit rating fell below investment grade for an IG index, or the entity defaulted) are removed.
  • New eligible entities are added to restore the index to its target number of constituents (125, 100, 75, etc.), chosen via a dealer poll based on CDS liquidity rankings.
  • The new series becomes the "on-the-run" (most liquid) contract, while older series continue to trade ("off-the-run") with progressively lower liquidity.
Remark — Fixed Coupon and Upfront

Like single-name CDS post-Big-Bang, index contracts trade with a fixed standardised running coupon (e.g., 100bp for CDX.NA.IG, 500bp for CDX.NA.HY), with the difference between the market-clearing index spread and this fixed coupon settled as an upfront payment — see Index Pricing.

Defaults Within the Index

If a constituent of the index experiences a credit event between roll dates, that name is settled via the standard CDS auction process and is removed from the index — the surviving notional is reduced by $1/N$ (where $N$ is the original number of constituents), and the protection seller pays the corresponding loss-given-default on that single name to the protection buyer. The index continues trading on its remaining constituents at the same fixed coupon, but its "factor" (the fraction of original notional remaining) drops below 1.
