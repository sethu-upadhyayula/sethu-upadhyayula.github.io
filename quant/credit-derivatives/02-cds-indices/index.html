2. CDS Indices

While single-name CDS reference the credit risk of one entity, CDS indices bundle a standardised basket of single-name CDS into a single, highly liquid contract. The two dominant families are CDX (North American and emerging market entities) and iTraxx (European and Asian entities), each with sub-indices covering investment-grade and high-yield constituents.

This chapter covers how CDS indices are constructed and rolled, and how the index spread relates to the spreads of its underlying constituents.

Remark - Liquidity and Use Cases

CDS index contracts are among the most liquid credit instruments in the market, often trading with tighter bid-offer spreads than the underlying single names combined. They are widely used by credit funds and dealers to hedge broad credit market exposure, by index-tranche desks as the reference portfolio for structured credit products (see Structured Credit), and as a benchmark for gauging overall credit market sentiment.
