4. Credit Derivative Structures Beyond Single-Name CDS

The first three chapters focused on the CDS contract itself — its mechanics, pricing, indices, and the curve-construction machinery that supports it. This final chapter surveys two other widely-used ways of transferring credit risk that build directly on the CDS framework but package it differently for different investor bases.

The total return swap (TRS) transfers both the credit risk and the funding/financing economics of an asset, making it a tool used as much for balance-sheet and funding management as for pure credit views. The credit-linked note (CLN) takes the opposite approach: it embeds a CDS inside a funded note, so that investors who cannot (or do not wish to) enter derivative contracts directly can still take on credit exposure through a familiar bond-like instrument.
