Credit Derivatives

Credit derivatives are instruments whose payoff depends on the creditworthiness of a reference entity, allowing market participants to transfer credit risk without transferring the underlying bond or loan. This section covers single-name credit default swaps (CDS), priced via the hazard rate framework, and CDS indices, which provide liquid, diversified exposure to broad credit markets.
