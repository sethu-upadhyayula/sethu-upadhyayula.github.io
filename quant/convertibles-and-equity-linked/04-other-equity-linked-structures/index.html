4. Other Equity-Linked Structures

The vanilla convertible bond of Chapters 1–3 — a fixed-coupon bond convertible at the holder's option into a fixed number of the issuer's own shares — is the simplest member of a broader family of equity-linked instruments. Issuers and structurers vary the conversion mechanics, the identity of the underlying stock, the principal protection, and the option style embedded in the structure to meet different financing or investment objectives.

This chapter surveys the most common variants. Section 4.1 covers mandatory convertibles, PEPS, and exchangeable bonds — instruments that modify, respectively, the optionality of conversion, the payoff profile at conversion, and the identity of the underlying stock. Section 4.2 turns to equity-linked notes and structured products sold primarily to investors rather than issued for corporate financing, including reverse convertibles and principal-protected notes, with a worked payoff example.
