3. Convertible Arbitrage and Greeks

The lattice and PDE machinery of Chapter 2 produces not just a price but a set of sensitivities — the Greeks — that describe how the convertible's value responds to small moves in the stock price, volatility, interest rates, and the issuer's credit spread. This chapter develops the delta and gamma of a convertible and shows how convertible arbitrage funds construct a delta-hedged position — long the convertible, short the delta-equivalent amount of stock — designed to profit from gamma trading (re-hedging as the stock moves) when the convertible's implied volatility is cheap relative to the stock's realised volatility.

The second part of the chapter extends the Greek framework to vega (volatility sensitivity) and credit-spread sensitivity (an analogue of CS01 for convertibles), and examines the cross-greeks that couple these exposures — most importantly, how a convertible's delta itself depends on the credit spread, given the credit-equity coupling introduced in Section 2.2. We close with a discussion of the risks that a delta-hedged convertible arbitrage book retains even when "fully hedged" on a model basis, using the 2008 convertible arbitrage unwind as a conceptual case study of how these risks can compound.
