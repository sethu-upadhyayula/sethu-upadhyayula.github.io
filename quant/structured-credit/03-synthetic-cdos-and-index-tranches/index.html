3. Synthetic CDOs and Index Tranches

Synthetic CDOs replace the cash bond or loan portfolio of a traditional CDO with a portfolio of credit default swaps, allowing tranches to be created and traded without funding the underlying notional. This chapter starts with the mechanics of synthetic CDO structures - how a CDS portfolio is tranched via attachment and detachment points, and how single-tranche CDOs let a dealer warehouse and hedge bespoke tranche exposure without distributing the entire capital structure.

The chapter then turns to the standardised index tranche market built on CDX (North America) and iTraxx (Europe and Asia) indices, covering market conventions for quoting tranche prices, how the base correlation surface from Chapter 2 is used to price both standard and bespoke tranches, and a worked example that ties together the loss distribution, base correlation interpolation, and premium/protection leg valuation into a single tranche price.
