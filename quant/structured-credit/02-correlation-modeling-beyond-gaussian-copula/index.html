2. Correlation Modeling Beyond Gaussian Copula

The one-factor Gaussian copula of Section 1.2 is computationally convenient but mechanically simplistic: a single correlation parameter cannot simultaneously reproduce the market prices of every tranche on an index. In practice, different tranches imply different "compound correlations," producing a correlation smile that has no analogue in a model with one free parameter. This chapter develops the base correlation framework that the market actually uses to interpolate and quote tranche prices consistently across the capital structure, and examines how implied correlation behaves across attachment points and through time.

It then looks beyond the Gaussian copula altogether, to multi-factor and alternative copula families - Student-$t$, double-$t$, and related constructions - that introduce tail dependence and richer correlation structures. These models address some of the well-documented shortcomings of the Gaussian framework, particularly its inability to generate the simultaneous large losses observed in systemic credit events, while remaining tractable enough for daily risk management of tranche books.
