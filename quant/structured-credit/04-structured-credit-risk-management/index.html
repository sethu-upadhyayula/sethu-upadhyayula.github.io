4. Structured Credit Risk Management

Running a structured credit book — whether single-tranche CDOs, index tranches, or bespoke positions — requires hedging the dominant risk factors day to day. This chapter starts with delta hedging: how a tranche's sensitivity to the underlying index spread is computed and offset by trading the index CDS itself, so that the desk's P&L is driven primarily by correlation and idiosyncratic views rather than directional credit moves.

The chapter then turns to the risks that delta hedging alone cannot remove — correlation risk, the sensitivity of tranche values to the correlation assumptions embedded in the base correlation surface of Chapter 2, and the broader model risk inherent in any copula-based pricing framework. We close with the lessons the 2008 financial crisis taught about correlation products: how correlation skew, liquidity, and model assumptions interacted to produce some of the largest losses of the crisis.
