3. Loan Portfolio Risk

Chapters 1 and 2 analysed single loans and mortgage pools in isolation. A lender's actual exposure, however, is a portfolio of thousands of loans with correlated default behaviour, varying credit quality, and concentrations by sector, geography, and borrower. This chapter moves from instrument-level mechanics to portfolio-level risk: how individual borrower creditworthiness is scored, how expected and unexpected losses aggregate across a portfolio, and how concentration risk shapes the tail of the loss distribution.

We then turn from risk measurement to risk-based pricing: how a lender translates expected loss, funding cost, and capital consumption into a required loan spread, and how risk-adjusted return on capital (RAROC) is used to compare loans of different risk profiles on a common footing. These portfolio-level tools are the foundation for the securitization structures developed in Chapter 4, where pools of loans are repackaged and their risk redistributed across tranches of investors.
