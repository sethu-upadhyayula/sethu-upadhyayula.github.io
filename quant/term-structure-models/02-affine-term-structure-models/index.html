2. Affine Term Structure Models

The Vasicek and Hull-White models of Chapter 1 are particular examples of a much broader class: affine term structure models, in which zero-coupon bond prices take the exponential-affine form $P(t,T) = \exp\big(A(t,T) - B(t,T)^{\top}X(t)\big)$ for some vector of state variables $X(t)$. This affine structure is not a coincidence - it arises whenever the drift and (squared) diffusion of the state variables are themselves affine functions of the state, and it is precisely this structure that makes closed-form bond and option prices possible.

This chapter generalises beyond the Gaussian short-rate setting. We first study the Cox-Ingersoll-Ross (CIR) model, whose square-root diffusion guarantees a non-negative short rate while preserving the affine bond-pricing structure. We then move to multi-factor affine models, which combine several state variables - typically interpreted as the level, slope, and curvature of the yield curve - to capture richer, more realistic curve dynamics than any single-factor model can produce.
