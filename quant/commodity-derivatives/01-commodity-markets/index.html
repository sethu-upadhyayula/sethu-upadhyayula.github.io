1. Commodity Markets

This chapter introduces the structure of physical commodity markets and the key concepts that distinguish them from purely financial markets. We examine the roles played by producers, consumers, intermediaries, and speculators, and develop the concept of convenience yield — the benefit of holding physical inventory — which is central to understanding the relationship between spot and futures prices.

The mechanics of spot price formation, the significance of physical delivery, and the information embedded in the shape of the futures curve all flow naturally from the institutional structure of commodity markets. These foundations underpin everything that follows: the cost-of-carry model, options pricing, and sector-specific analysis.
