3. Swap Risk and Hedging

With multi-curve valuation established (Chapter 2), the next question is risk: how does the value of a swap respond to changes in market rates, and how is that risk managed in practice? This chapter introduces PV01 (also called DV01), the standard measure of a swap's sensitivity to a one-basis-point move in rates, and shows how that sensitivity decomposes across the discounting curve and the relevant forecasting curve in the multi-curve setting.

It then turns to the practical problem facing any trading desk: a book of many swaps with offsetting and non-offsetting risks across maturities and curves. The chapter covers how such books are hedged using other swaps and futures, the distinction between bucket hedging and key-rate hedging, and the residual curve risk that remains even after a book is hedged to zero net PV01.
