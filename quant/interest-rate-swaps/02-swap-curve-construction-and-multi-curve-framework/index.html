2. Swap Curve Construction and the Multi-Curve Framework

Chapter 1 introduced the multi-curve framework conceptually: one curve for discounting cash flows, and separate curves for projecting the floating rates referenced by each tenor of swap. This chapter makes that framework operational. It covers how the SOFR/OIS discount curve and tenor-specific forecast curves are bootstrapped together from market-quoted instruments — vanilla swaps, basis swaps, and futures — so that every leg of every swap can be valued consistently with observed market prices.

The chapter also examines tenor basis swaps directly, since they are both an important traded product in their own right and the primary calibration instrument that links forecast curves of different tenors to one another. Understanding why tenor basis exists, what drives its level, and how it is priced is essential background for anyone building or using a multi-curve system.
