Instruments

Carnatic music is fundamentally vocal in conception - even purely instrumental performances typically aim to emulate the phrasing and gamaka of vocal music (Chapter 1 of Improvisation & Ornamentation) - but a distinctive family of instruments has developed to support, accompany, and sometimes substitute for the voice. This subject covers the voice itself, the melodic accompaniment and solo instruments, and the percussion ensemble that anchors a performance's rhythmic framework (The Tāla System).
