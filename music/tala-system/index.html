The Tāla System

Tāla is Carnatic music's rhythmic counterpart to rāga - a cyclic framework of beats that a composition (Musical Forms & Compositions) is set to and that improvisation (Improvisation & Ornamentation) must stay anchored within. This subject covers the physical hand gestures used to keep tāla, the formal scheme classifying tāla types, how each beat can be further subdivided, and konnakol, the spoken rhythmic solfège used to internalize it all.
