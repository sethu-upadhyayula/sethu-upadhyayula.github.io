2. Aṅga, Akṣara, and Kriya

A tāla cycle is built from a small set of named component sections, each corresponding to a specific physical hand gesture - this chapter covers both the abstract structure and its physical, gestural realization.

Akṣara: The Basic Time Unit

An akṣara is the basic counting unit of a tāla - roughly analogous to a "beat," though its actual duration is not fixed in absolute time but set by the performance's chosen tempo (kāla), the same beat framework simply passing faster or slower depending on how briskly a piece is performed.

The Three Aṅgas

The Three Building-Block Aṅgas
  • Anudruta - a single akṣara, marked with one clap.
  • Druta - two akṣaras, marked with one clap followed by a wave of the hand (or a "turn" of the palm).
  • Laghu - a variable number of akṣaras (its count, the jāti, can be 3, 4, 5, 7, or 9 depending on the tāla, Chapter 3), marked with one clap followed by counting on the fingers.

A tāla's full cycle is defined as a specific ordered sequence of these three aṅgas - for example, one laghu followed by one druta - and the tāla's total akṣara count (and hence its overall length/duration per cycle) is simply the sum of the akṣaras contributed by each aṅga in that sequence.

Kriya: The Physical Gestures

Definition - Kriya

Kriya refers to the physical hand gestures - claps, waves, and finger counts - used to mark and keep track of a tāla cycle in real time. Because each aṅga has its own specific gesture (above), a performer's hand movements directly and visibly trace the tāla's structure moment to moment, making kriya both a practical timekeeping tool and, for an audience member who knows the tāla, a visible readout of exactly where in the cycle a performance currently stands.

Kriya in Learning and Teaching

Students are taught to physically perform kriya from very early in their training, well before attempting the melodic material a given tāla will accompany - internalizing the rhythmic cycle as a physical, embodied habit is considered foundational, not an optional add-on to melodic learning.
