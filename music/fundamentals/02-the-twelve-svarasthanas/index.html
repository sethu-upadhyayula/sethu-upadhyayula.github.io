2. The Twelve Svarasthānas

Within one octave above the śruti (Chapter 1), Carnatic music recognizes twelve distinct pitch positions - svarasthānas - the raw material every rāga (Chapter 2 of The Rāga System) is built from.

The Seven Svara Names

Definition - The Seven Svaras

Carnatic music names seven basic svaras: Sa, Ri (Ṛṣabha), Ga (Gāndhāra), Ma (Madhyama), Pa (Pañcama), Da (Dhaivata), Ni (Niṣāda) - directly analogous in function to the Western solfège Do-Re-Mi-Fa-Sol-La-Ti, and to Hindustani music's Sa-Re-Ga-Ma-Pa-Dha-Ni. Sa and Pa are fixed, invariant positions in every rāga; the other five each have variant forms, described below.

From Seven Names to Twelve Positions

While there are only seven svara names, Ri, Ga, Ma, Da, and Ni each have more than one possible pitch position (their variants are numbered, e.g. Ri1/Ri2/Ri3), while Sa and Pa have exactly one fixed position each - together yielding exactly twelve distinct svarasthānas across the octave, matching the twelve semitone positions of a chromatic scale, though Carnatic theory arrives at and organizes them independently of Western equal temperament.

Variant Svaras
  • Ri (Ṛṣabha) - three positions: Ri1, Ri2, Ri3.
  • Ga (Gāndhāra) - three positions: Ga1, Ga2, Ga3.
  • Ma (Madhyama) - two positions: Ma1 (śuddha madhyama), Ma2 (prati madhyama).
  • Da (Dhaivata) - three positions: Da1, Da2, Da3.
  • Ni (Niṣāda) - three positions: Ni1, Ni2, Ni3.
Remark - Overlapping Positions

Some variant positions of neighbouring svaras coincide at the same actual pitch (for instance, Ri2 and Ga1 can fall on the same svarasthāna) - which specific name is used in a given rāga depends on that rāga's own construction (Chapter 2 of The Rāga System), not on the raw pitch position alone. This is analogous to enharmonic spelling in Western notation (the same key on a piano being called either D♯ or E♭ depending on context).

Śuddha and Vikṛta Svaras

Svara positions are also classified as śuddha ("natural"/unaltered) or vikṛta ("altered") relative to a reference scale - terminology that connects the practical twelve-position system covered here back to the classical microtonal (22-śruti) framework mentioned in Chapter 1.
