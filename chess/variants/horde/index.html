Horde Chess

Horde Chess is an asymmetric variant pitting White's army of 36 pawns - a relentless, advancing horde - against Black's normal chess pieces. White wins by checkmating Black's king. Black wins by capturing every single White pawn. No other variant so dramatically illustrates the tension between massed quantity and organised quality.

The four chapters below cover the unusual starting position and rules unique to the Horde, the strategic approach for each side, and a discussion of how balanced the variant really is along with practical tips for fast time controls.

2. White's Strategy: The Advance

3. Black's Strategy: Thinning the Horde

Creating passed pawns, exploiting knight outposts, and endgame counting against the remaining mass.

Is Horde Chess balanced? Time pressure dynamics and recommended opening moves for both sides.
