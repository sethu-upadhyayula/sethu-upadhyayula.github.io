Chess Variants

Variants alter one or more rules of standard chess to produce games with distinct tactical and strategic character — different win conditions, different piece behaviour, or entirely different starting positions. Each of the eight variants below has its own page covering the rules, the key differences from classical chess, and the ideas and traps specific to that game.

Antichess

The goal is to lose all your pieces (or reach stalemate). Captures are mandatory when available.

Atomic Chess

Captures cause explosions that destroy all pieces on surrounding squares. The king can never be in check in the usual sense.

Chess 960

Fischer Random Chess: the back rank pieces are shuffled randomly (960 possible positions), eliminating opening preparation.

Crazyhouse

Captured pieces switch sides and can be dropped back onto the board as a move. Material counts and dynamics change completely.

Horde

White has 36 pawns and no other pieces. Black wins by capturing all White pawns; White wins by checkmating the Black king.

King of the Hill

Win by checkmating the opponent or by moving your king to one of the four central squares (e4, d4, e5, d5).

Racing Kings

Both sides race their king to the eighth rank. No checks are allowed — every move must avoid placing the opponent's king in check.

Three Check

Win by checkmating the opponent or by giving check three times. King safety takes on a completely different character.
