Atomic Chess

In Atomic Chess, every capture triggers an explosion that annihilates the capturing piece and all non-pawn pieces on adjacent squares. One wrong capture can wipe out an entire army — or win the game in a single move.

Atomic Chess is one of the most popular variants on Lichess, prized for its explosive tactics and dramatically shortened games. The four chapters below take you from the core explosion mechanics through the inverted strategic principles that govern king safety, the signature tactical patterns that decide most games, and finally the opening theory and practical tips you need to start winning with (and against) atomic bombs on the board.

1. The Rules of Atomic Chess

Explosion mechanics, the king exception, and how games are won and lost.

2. King Safety and the Inversion of Values

Why the king is both the most fragile and most aggressive piece, pawn shields, and connected kings.

3. Atomic Tactics

Knight and rook explosions, sacrificial chain reactions, and avoiding self-destruction.

4. Opening Theory and Practical Tips

Adjusted opening principles, famous traps, and endgame considerations.
