Racing Kings

Racing Kings strips chess down to its purest race: both players start on the bottom two ranks with all pieces except pawns, and the goal is simply to be the first to move your king to the 8th rank. There are no pawns, no checks allowed, and every move must leave the opponent's king completely safe. It is chess as a sprint.

The four chapters below cover the unusual starting position and the all-important no-check rule, the strategic ideas that govern the race (clearing paths and blocking the opponent), the tactical motifs that decide close races, and practical tips for opening play and endgame calculation.

1. Rules of Racing Kings

The starting position, the no-check rule, and how a game is won or drawn.

2. Strategic Ideas

Racing your king forward, clearing paths, and using pieces as blockers.

3. Tactical Motifs

Tempo, zugzwang, race calculations, deflection, and the check-block paradox.

4. Practical Tips and Typical Patterns

Opening priorities, the Black draw mechanism, and endgame race technique.
