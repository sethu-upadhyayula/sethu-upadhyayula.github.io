Antichess (Losing Chess)

Antichess turns chess completely upside down: the goal is to lose all your pieces, or be stalemated. Captures are mandatory when available, the king has no special status, and every piece you still own at the end is a liability. It is one of the most deeply solved and theoretically rich variants in existence.

The four chapters below take you from the basic rule changes through the inverted strategy and tactics that define the variant, all the way to the surprising paradoxes and the computer-verified theory of perfect play. Antichess (also known as Losing Chess, Suicide Chess, or Giveaway Chess) rewards exactly the same calculation skills as regular chess — just pointed in the opposite direction.
