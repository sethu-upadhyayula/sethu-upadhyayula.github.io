King of the Hill

King of the Hill adds a second winning condition to standard chess: march your king to the centre of the board. This single rule transforms the most timid piece in chess into a bold, marauding attacker — and turns every game into a frantic race for the four central squares.

The four chapters below cover the rules and the precise definition of the hill win, the strategic inversion of king safety that the variant creates, the tactical patterns unique to the king race, and practical advice for opening, middlegame, and endgame play.

1. Rules and Winning Conditions

The hill squares, the simultaneous-threat caveat, and how King of the Hill differs from standard chess.

2. Strategic Impact: The King Race

Why king safety is inverted, how both sides race for the centre, and the doubled importance of central control.

3. Tactical Patterns

The king march, blocking and deflection, and the constant tension between mating attacks and the hill race.

4. Typical Game Patterns and Practical Advice

Opening principles, endgame considerations, and how the hill race balances material imbalances.
