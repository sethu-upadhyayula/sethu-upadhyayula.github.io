Chess Compositions

Composed chess problems are positions crafted by composers to achieve a specified goal — a mate in a set number of moves, a draw, a proof game, or something more exotic. Each of the eight genres below has its own conventions, solving techniques, and aesthetic ideals, from the elegance of a two-move mate to the deductive puzzle of a retro-analysis problem.

Twomovers

White mates in exactly 2 moves. The richest genre of direct mates, with extensive theory around keys, threats, tries, and themes.

Threemovers

White mates in exactly 3 moves. The extra move introduces continuations, plan changes, and more elaborate thematic content.

Moremovers

Mate in n moves for n ≥ 4. Includes strategic plans, long manoeuvres, and complex branching variations.

Endgame Studies

Composed positions with a realistic feel where White wins or draws by force, often by means of a surprising and instructive manoeuvre.

Selfmates

White forces Black to deliver checkmate in exactly n moves. White plays to be mated; Black resists.

Helpmates

Black moves first and cooperates with White; both sides work together so that White delivers checkmate on the final move.

Retro Problems

Proof games, shortest proof games, enumerations, and other problems requiring retrograde analysis to determine what moves were played.

Fairies

Problems using non-standard pieces (nightriders, grasshoppers, etc.) or non-standard conditions (circe, madrasi, grid chess, and more).
