Twomovers

The twomover — White to play and mate in exactly 2 moves against any Black defence — is the most intensively studied genre of chess composition. Its brevity demands absolute precision: every Black move must be answered, and every answer must be checkmate.

The six chapters below build from the basic stipulation up to the artistic standards used to judge the finest examples in the genre. You will meet the vocabulary of keys, threats, variations, and tries; the classical themes (Indian, Bristol, battery play, Novotny interference) that recur across thousands of compositions; a fully worked classic problem with diagram and solution; a systematic method for solving twomovers over the board or on paper; and the aesthetic criteria — economy, unity, surprise, and purity of mate — that separate a good twomover from a great one.

1. Definition and Stipulation

The #2 stipulation, the key move, and why Black never cooperates.

2. Key Concepts

Keys, threats, zugzwang, variations, tries, and changed play.

3. Classical Themes

Indian theme, Bristol clearance, battery play, Novotny, and pins.

4. A Classic Problem

A fully diagrammed battery twomover with complete solution.

5. How to Solve Twomovers

A systematic method: survey, set play, batteries, quiet keys, verification.

6. Aesthetic Criteria

Economy, unity, surprise, rich tries, changed play, and purity of mate.
