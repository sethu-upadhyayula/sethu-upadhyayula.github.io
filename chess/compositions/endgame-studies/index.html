Endgame Studies

Endgame studies occupy a unique position in chess art: composed positions that look like they could arise in real play, where White wins or draws by force through a sequence of moves that surprises and instructs. The greatest studies reveal truths about chess that no practical game could teach as cleanly.

The six chapters below define what an endgame study is and how it differs from a chess problem, survey the great composers of the study form, work through the most celebrated study of all time move by move, explore Troitzky's landmark analysis of K+2N vs K+P, and finish with practical advice for solving and appreciating studies.

1. Definition

What an endgame study is: the win/draw stipulation, the absence of a move count, and the criterion of surprise.

2. Studies vs. Problems

How studies differ from directmates and other problems, and why they sit between art and practical training.

3. Major Composers

Rinck, Troitzky, Réti, Kling & Horwitz, Dvoretsky, and the traditions they founded.

4. The Réti Study (1921)

A move-by-move walkthrough of the most famous endgame study ever composed, and the geometric idea behind it.

5. Troitzky's K+2N vs K+P

How an extra Black pawn turns a drawn K+2N ending into a win, and the Troitzky Line that maps the boundary.

6. Solving and Appreciating Studies

A practical method for approaching studies, and why they remain unmatched training material.
