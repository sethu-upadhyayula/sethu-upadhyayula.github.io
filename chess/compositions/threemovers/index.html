Threemovers

The threemover (#3) adds one full move of play to the twomover, and that single extra move opens a vastly richer universe of themes. White's key sets up a threat; Black defends; White plays a continuation that sets up a mating threat on move 3; Black defends again; and White mates. Each decision point multiplies the combinatorial complexity, and the threemover becomes the natural home for strategic plans, batteries-in-waiting, and the logical "foreplan and mainplan" structure.

The six chapters below build up the threemover from first principles: what the stipulation #3 actually requires, how the genre differs structurally from the twomover, the classical themes (Nowotny, Grimshaw, half-battery, Turton, logical problems) that recur across the literature, a fully worked illustrative example with diagrams, a systematic solving method, and the aesthetic standards by which threemovers are judged.
