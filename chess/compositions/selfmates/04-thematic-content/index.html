4. Thematic Content

Many of the named themes from directmate composition reappear in the selfmate world - but each one undergoes a transformation, because the final beneficiary of the mechanism is Black's piece, not White's. This chapter surveys four such themes and discusses how their "reversed" versions feel different from their directmate originals.

The Selfmate Nowotny

In a directmate Nowotny, White sacrifices a piece on the intersection square of a Black rook's and a Black bishop's lines of defence. Whichever piece captures, it abandons the guard duty the other piece was relying on it not to abandon - and White mates accordingly, exploiting whichever line is now vacated.

In a selfmate, the same intersection-sacrifice idea is repurposed: White sacrifices on the intersection of two Black piece lines, but now the point of the sacrifice is to force one specific Black piece to capture and thereby move to the intersection square - from which it can deliver checkmate on the White king. Both possible Black captures might lead to mate (a "dual" outcome that is sometimes the central point of the problem, with each capture producing a different model mate), or only one capture might work, with the other refuted by some other consideration. Either way, the Nowotny's "choice between two captures, both fatal" flavour survives the inversion - it is just fatal to White's king rather than Black's.

Rundlauf (Round Trip)

A Rundlauf is a manoeuvre in which a piece - White or Black - travels in a loop, visiting a sequence of squares and eventually returning to its starting square (or to a square on its original path), often spanning several moves of a longer problem. In directmates, Rundlaufs are prized for their "wasted effort that wasn't wasted" quality: the piece appears to go nowhere, but its journey was essential to the final position.

In selfmates, a Rundlauf often belongs to a White piece that must execute a precise circuit purely to arrive back at (or near) its starting square having, along the way, forced Black's king or another Black piece into a slightly different configuration - enough to convert what would otherwise be a Black escape into the desired zugzwang. The Rundlauf is thus a kind of "tempo manoeuvre with side effects": White spends moves that look like they accomplish nothing geometrically, while quietly engineering the precise zugzwang needed for the final mate.

Model Mates - With Black Holding the Knife

A model mate is a mating position in which every piece taking part in the mate performs a distinct function (checking, or guarding an escape square) with no duplication. In directmates, this is a property of White's mating moves. In selfmates, the mating piece is always a Black piece - so a selfmate model mate is a position where Black's checking piece, together with the remaining Black pieces (and the position of the White king itself), combine so that the White king has no flight square, with no redundant guards.

This is part of what makes selfmate model mates particularly prized: the composer must engineer an economical mating net using the opponent's own army, arranged through a sequence of moves where the opponent was actively (if unsuccessfully) trying to avoid contributing to exactly that net. The Bristol Clearance in Selfmates

A Bristol is a long-range clearance: a piece travels along a line, all the way to the far end, purely to get out of the way so that another piece of the same colour can use that line. In directmate Bristols, a White piece clears a line for another White piece to deliver mate.

In a selfmate, the clearance is performed by White - but the line being cleared is one that a Black piece will use to deliver checkmate. White's piece travels the full length of a rank, file, or diagonal specifically so that the corresponding Black piece's path to the White king is no longer blocked. The clearance is therefore White's deliberate act of making way for its own executioner: White removes the one obstacle that was (perhaps) the only thing preventing Black's piece from being forced to deliver the fatal check.

Why Reused Themes Still Feel Fresh

It might seem like reusing directmate themes in selfmates is merely "the same trick with the colours swapped" - but the experience of solving (and appreciating) a selfmate built on one of these themes is genuinely different. In a directmate Nowotny, the solver is looking for how White exploits a weakness in Black's defensive geometry. In a selfmate Nowotny, the solver must additionally verify that the forced capture is truly forced - that Black has no alternative defence - and that the resulting mate is inescapable from Black's perspective despite Black's best efforts. The extra layer of "and Black really couldn't avoid this" verification is what gives reused themes their distinct selfmate character.
