Selfmates

In a selfmate, White plays to force Black to deliver checkmate - while Black does everything possible to avoid doing so. Every natural chess instinct is inverted: White sacrifices aggressively, creates threats against its own king, and drives Black pieces into position to deliver the fatal blow.

The six chapters below introduce the selfmate genre from first principles: what the stipulation means, why the logic feels so backwards at first, the standard techniques composers use to engineer a forced mate-by-Black, the thematic families that recur across the literature, a fully worked example with a diagram and solution, and a step-by-step method for solving selfmates yourself.

1. Definition

The s#n stipulation, the reluctant-executioner asymmetry, and how selfmates fit into the wider world of chess composition.

2. Why Selfmates Are Counterintuitive

Reversing every chess instinct: attacking your own king, sacrificing on purpose, and the constant tension with stalemate.

3. Key Techniques

Zugzwang, pin exploitation, controlled captures, reversed batteries, and stalemate avoidance - the composer's core toolkit.

4. Thematic Content

The selfmate Nowotny, Rundlauf, model mates, and the Bristol clearance - classic themes reimagined for the genre.

5. An Illustrative Selfmate

A fully worked s#2 miniature with a diagram, move-by-move solution, and discussion of why every Black defence fails.

6. How to Solve Selfmates

A practical, step-by-step method for working backward from the mating position to the key move.
