Moremovers

Moremovers — problems with the stipulation #n where n ≥ 4 — occupy the frontier between the purely combinatorial world of short problems and the strategic world of endgame studies. They demand plan-based thinking, long manoeuvres, and an ability to see positional motifs unfolding across many moves.

The six chapters below build from the basic definition and scope of the moremover, through the characteristics that distinguish it from shorter problems, the composers who shaped the genre, a fully worked knight-manoeuvre problem, the artistic distinction between moremovers and endgame studies, and finally a practical method for solving moremovers at the board.

1. Definition and Scope

What #n means for n ≥ 4, and how the moremover sits between the twomover and the endgame study.

2. Characteristics

Plan-based thinking, long manoeuvres, zugzwang, positional motifs, and the logical theme at scale.

3. Famous Composers and Their Styles

Sam Loyd, Henry Dudeney, Comins Mansfield, Werner Speckmann, and the schools they founded.

4. A Knight Manoeuvre Problem

A fully worked #5 built around a long knight tour, with diagram and complete solution.

5. Moremovers vs. Endgame Studies

A side-by-side look at stipulation, appearance, and aesthetic criteria across the two genres.

6. How to Solve Moremovers

A step-by-step method: identify the plan, find the main line, work backwards from the mate.
