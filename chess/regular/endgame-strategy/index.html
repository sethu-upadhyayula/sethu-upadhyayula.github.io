Endgame Strategy

The endgame is where games are decided, yet it is the phase most often neglected in study. While Endgame Theory catalogues the precise positions you must know cold — King and Pawn endings, Lucena and Philidor positions, and the like — endgame strategy is about the broader thinking that applies across all endgames: how to evaluate an endgame, what general principles guide play with reduced material, and how to convert an advantage (or hold a disadvantage) when the board has emptied out.

The seven chapters below build from general endgame principles through evaluation, zugzwang, converting advantages, and piece-specific strategy, finishing with how to think about the transition from the middlegame into the endgame.

1. General Principles of the Endgame

King activity, the principle of two weaknesses, and not rushing.

2. Evaluating an Endgame

Material, pawn structure, king activity, piece activity, and outside passed pawns.

3. Zugzwang

The compulsion to move, the opposition, and triangulation.

4. Converting an Advantage

Simplification, denying counterplay, and the fortress.

5. Minor Piece Endgame Strategy

Same vs opposite-coloured bishops, knight vs bishop, and the wrong-coloured bishop.

6. Rook Endgame Strategy

Activity over material, rooks behind passed pawns, and cutting off the king.

7. Transitioning into the Endgame

Calculating the resulting endgame and trading the right pieces.
