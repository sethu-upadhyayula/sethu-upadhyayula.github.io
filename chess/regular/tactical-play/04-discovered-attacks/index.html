4. Discovered Attacks

A discovered attack occurs when one piece moves, uncovering an attack from another piece behind it. Both pieces can simultaneously create threats, making discovered attacks extremely powerful because the opponent must deal with two threats at once.

Discovered Check

When the piece behind the moving piece is attacking the king, the result is a discovered check. The moving piece is free to go anywhere — including capturing another piece — while the opponent must deal with the discovered check. This is one of the most devastating tactics in chess.

Double Check

The most powerful tactical weapon: double check occurs when the moving piece also gives check in addition to the discovered check. The king is simultaneously attacked by two pieces. There is only one way to escape: the king must move. Blocking is impossible (would only block one check), and capturing is impossible (cannot capture two pieces in one move). Double check is notated with ++.

Moving the knight on d6 creates a discovered attack from the White rook on b1 against the Black bishop on b8

Key Principle

When you spot a piece that is lined up behind one of your own pieces along a rank, file, or diagonal, always consider moving the front piece to simultaneously attack or create a second threat. The combination is especially powerful when the front piece moves with check or captures something valuable.
