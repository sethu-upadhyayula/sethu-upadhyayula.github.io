6. Decoy (Attraction)

A decoy (or attraction) tactic lures an enemy piece — most often the king — to a specific square where it can be exploited. Unlike deflection (which removes a defender), decoy positively attracts the target piece to a bad position.

Classic queen sacrifice pattern: White plays Qxh7+!, sacrificing the queen. Black must capture: Kxh7. Now a double check or a fork delivers checkmate or wins decisive material. The king has been attracted to h7, exactly where the mating net is ready.

King Decoys

Decoying the king is especially powerful because the king's movements are restricted — once lured to a bad square, it is often permanently exposed. Common decoy patterns include:

  • Sacrificing a rook on h8 to lure the Black king from g8 to h8, then delivering a knight fork or bishop check
  • Queen sacrifices that lure the king to the front of its own pawns, enabling a smothered mate
  • Sacrifices on f7 (the weakest square early in the game) that drag the king into the centre
