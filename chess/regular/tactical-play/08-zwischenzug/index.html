8. Zwischenzug (In-Between Move)

A zwischenzug ("in-between move," also called an intermezzo) is a forcing move — typically a check, capture, or serious threat — inserted before an "expected" recapture or response. The opponent must address the zwischenzug first, often changing the evaluation of the entire sequence.

Classic pattern: White's bishop on e3 is attacked by Black's knight on d5 (Nxe3). Instead of immediately recapturing, White inserts a zwischenzug such as Bxh7+!, winning a pawn with check before dealing with the original exchange. After Kxh7, White recaptures fxe3 (or similar), having gained a "free" tempo and pawn.

Why Zwischenzugs Are Easy to Miss

Players often calculate a sequence assuming both sides will play the "natural" recapture immediately. A zwischenzug breaks this assumption — it requires noticing that a forcing move exists before the expected reply, and that this move improves the position enough to justify delaying the recapture. Always ask: "Before I recapture, is there a check, capture, or major threat available?"

Zwischenzug in Defence

Zwischenzugs are not only attacking weapons. A defender facing an apparently forced loss of material can sometimes interpose a counter-threat of their own — a check or a bigger capture — that forces the attacker to respond first, buying time to reorganise the defence.
