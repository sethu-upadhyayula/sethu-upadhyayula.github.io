Opening Theory

The opening is the first phase of a chess game, typically lasting 10–15 moves. Strong opening play sets the foundation for the entire game — good openings lead to good middlegame positions. While memorising long opening lines is unnecessary for beginners, understanding key principles and popular opening systems is invaluable at every level.

The six chapters below cover how openings are classified, the core principles that apply regardless of which opening you choose, the major opening families for both 1.e4 and 1.d4, flank/hypermodern systems, and a practical framework for building your own repertoire.
