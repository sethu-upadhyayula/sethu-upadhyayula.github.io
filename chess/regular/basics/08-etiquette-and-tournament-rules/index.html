8. Etiquette and Tournament Rules

Chess has a long tradition of etiquette, much of which is now codified in the official rules used in over-the-board tournaments (governed internationally by FIDE, the World Chess Federation).

Touch-Move Rule

In tournament play, if a player deliberately touches one of their own pieces, they must move that piece if it has a legal move (the "touch-move" rule). If a player touches an opponent's piece, they must capture it if a legal capture is possible ("touch-take"). Players who wish to adjust a piece on its square without being bound by touch-move must first announce "j'adoube" ("I adjust") before touching it.

Recording Moves

In standard time controls, both players are required to record every move on a scoresheet using algebraic notation. This serves to verify the result, settle disputes (e.g., claims of threefold repetition or the 50-move rule), and produce a record of the game for later study.

The Clock

Tournament games use a chess clock with two displays — one for each player's remaining time. After making a move, a player presses the clock to start the opponent's clock. Common time controls include "classical" (e.g., 90 minutes + 30-second increment per move), "rapid" (15–25 minutes), and "blitz" (3–5 minutes). Running out of time on the clock loses the game (unless the opponent has insufficient material to ever checkmate, in which case it is a draw).

General Conduct

  • Offer a handshake before and after the game.
  • Resign gracefully when the position is hopeless — playing on in a completely lost position against a much stronger opponent is considered poor form, though it is always a player's right to continue.
  • Do not talk, analyse out loud, or distract your opponent during the game.
  • Mobile phones must be switched off; in many federations, having a phone ring during a game results in an automatic loss.
  • Draw offers should be made only after making a move, and only once per "reasonable" interval — repeatedly offering draws to annoy an opponent is against the spirit of the rules.
The Golden Rule: If you touch it, you move it. If you can take it, you take it. Always think before you touch a piece!
