3. Special Rules

Castling

Castling is a special move involving the king and a rook. The king moves two squares toward a rook, and the rook jumps over the king to the square on the king's other side. It is the only move in chess where two pieces move simultaneously.

Kingside Castling (0-0)

King moves from e1 to g1; rook moves from h1 to f1. (For Black: e8 to g8, rook h8 to f8.)

Queenside Castling (0-0-0)

King moves from e1 to c1; rook moves from a1 to d1. (For Black: e8 to c8, rook a8 to d8.)

Conditions for Castling

  • Neither the king nor the chosen rook has previously moved
  • There are no pieces between the king and the rook
  • The king is not currently in check
  • The king does not pass through a square that is attacked by an opponent's piece
  • The king does not land on a square that is in check

Note: The rook may pass through an attacked square; only the king's path matters.

White can castle both sides: king on e1, rooks on a1 and h1

En Passant

En passant (French: "in passing") is a special pawn capture. If a pawn advances two squares from its starting position and lands beside an opponent's pawn, the opponent's pawn may capture it as if it had only moved one square. This capture must be made immediately on the very next move — the right to capture en passant expires after one move.

Example: White pawn on e5, Black plays d7–d5 (landing on d5 next to the e5 pawn). White may capture: exd6, with the white pawn ending up on d6 and the black pawn on d5 removed from the board.

En passant: White pawn on e5, Black just played d7–d5. White can capture exd6.

Promotion

When a pawn reaches the opposite end of the board (the 8th rank for White, 1st rank for Black), it must be promoted to another piece: queen, rook, bishop, or knight. The player chooses which piece. Promotion to a queen (called "queening") is by far the most common choice, as the queen is the most powerful piece. Occasionally, promoting to a knight ("underpromotion") is the correct move to avoid stalemate or to deliver an immediate fork.
