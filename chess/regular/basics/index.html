Basics of Chess

Chess is a two-player strategy board game played on an 8×8 grid. Understanding the board, the pieces, and the fundamental rules is the starting point for every chess journey — from beginner to grandmaster.

The eight chapters below cover everything you need to start playing: the board and pieces, special rules like castling and en passant, how games end, how to read and write chess moves, and the etiquette expected at the board.

1. The Board

The 8x8 grid, files, ranks, squares, and the central squares.

2. The Pieces

The six piece types, their values, movement, and the knight's special movement.

3. Special Rules

Castling, en passant, and pawn promotion.

4. Check, Checkmate, Stalemate, and Draws

How the game ends: check, checkmate, stalemate, and the rules for a draw.

5. Algebraic Notation

Reading and writing chess moves in standard algebraic notation.

6. FEN and PGN

Recording positions (FEN) and full games (PGN) in standard formats.

7. An Introduction to Mating Patterns

Common mating patterns every beginner should recognise on sight.

8. Etiquette and Tournament Rules

Touch-move, the clock, recording moves, and tournament conduct.
