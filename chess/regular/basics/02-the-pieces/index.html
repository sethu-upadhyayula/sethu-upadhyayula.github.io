2. The Pieces

Each player starts with 16 pieces: 1 king, 1 queen, 2 rooks, 2 bishops, 2 knights, and 8 pawns. White always moves first.

PieceSymbolValueStarting SquaresMovement
King ♔ ♚ ∞ (priceless) e1 / e8 One square in any direction (horizontally, vertically, or diagonally). Cannot move into check. Most important piece - losing it means losing the game.
Queen ♕ ♛ ~9 points d1 / d8 Any number of squares along a rank, file, or diagonal. The most powerful piece on the board. Combines rook and bishop movement.
Rook ♖ ♜ ~5 points a1, h1 / a8, h8 Any number of squares horizontally or vertically (along ranks and files). Cannot jump over pieces. Most powerful in open files and the 7th rank.
Bishop ♗ ♝ ~3 points c1, f1 / c8, f8 Any number of squares diagonally. Always remains on the same colour it started on. One bishop covers light squares, the other covers dark squares.
Knight ♘ ♞ ~3 points b1, g1 / b8, g8 L-shaped: 2 squares in one direction, then 1 square perpendicular (or vice versa). The only piece that can jump over other pieces. Always lands on the opposite colour from its starting square.
Pawn ♙ ♟ ~1 point a2-h2 / a7-h7 Moves forward one square (or two squares from its starting position on the 2nd/7th rank). Captures diagonally forward one square. Cannot move backward. The "soul of chess" (Philidor).

Material Values and Exchanges

The material values above are rough guides for evaluating exchanges. Key principles:

  • Queen (~9) ≈ Rook (~5) + Bishop (~3) + one pawn (with some positional bonus)
  • Trading a rook for a bishop or knight is called "winning/losing the exchange"
  • Two bishops together (the "bishop pair") are often worth slightly more than a bishop and knight
  • In the endgame, the king becomes an active piece and should be used aggressively

The Knight's Movement

The knight moves in an L-shape: exactly 2 squares in one orthogonal direction, then 1 square perpendicular. From the centre of the board (e.g., d4), a knight controls 8 squares. From the corner (a1), it only controls 2. This is why "A knight on the rim is dim" - always aim to centralise your knights.
