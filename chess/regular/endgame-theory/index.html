Endgame Theory

Theoretical endgames are positions whose outcome — win, draw, or loss — has been precisely worked out, often centuries ago, and whose techniques apply whenever the same material balance arises. Knowing these positions cold means you can steer toward a known win or a known draw with confidence, rather than calculating everything from scratch under time pressure. The eight chapters below cover the core theoretical material balances every serious player should know.

1. King and Pawn Endgames

Opposition, key squares, the rule of the square, and the a/h-pawn exception.

2. Rook Endgames

The Lucena and Philidor positions, active rooks, and the Vancura position.

3. Minor Piece Endgames

Bishop vs knight, opposite-coloured bishops, and queen endgames.

4. Checkmate Techniques

K+Q, K+R, K+2B, K+B+N, K+P, and the K+2N vs K+P exception.

5. Queen Endgames

K+Q vs K+P drawing chances, queen vs queen and perpetual check, and Q+P vs Q technique.

6. Fortresses and Drawing Techniques

The wrong-coloured bishop, blocked structures, rook fortresses, and general drawing resources.

7. Zugzwang, Triangulation, and Corresponding Squares

Zugzwang formalised, triangulation to transfer the move, and corresponding squares.

8. Material-Imbalance Endgames

Two major pieces vs a lone king, queen vs rook, queen vs minor piece, and rook vs minor piece.
