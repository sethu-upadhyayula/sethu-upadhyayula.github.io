Strategic Play

While tactics are short-term and forcing, strategy is the art of long-term planning. Strategic thinking identifies the structural features of a position — pawn weaknesses, piece activity, square control, king safety — and formulates a plan to exploit them over many moves. As Grandmaster Savielly Tartakower put it: "Tactics flow from a superior position." Strategy is what creates that superior position.

The nine chapters below cover pawn structure, weak squares and outposts, piece activity, prophylaxis, Silman's imbalances, the principle of two weaknesses, the isolated queen's pawn, exchange sacrifices, and how to formulate a concrete plan.

1. Pawn Structure

Isolated, doubled, backward, and passed pawns, pawn chains, and pawn breaks.

2. Weak Squares and Outposts

Identifying weak squares, creating outposts, and the good/bad bishop rule.

3. Piece Activity

Open files, the 7th rank, knight centralisation, and the worst-placed piece.

4. Prophylaxis

Anticipating and preventing your opponent's plans before they happen.

5. Imbalances

Silman's seven imbalances and how to use them to form a plan.

6. The Principle of Two Weaknesses

Why two weaknesses on opposite wings cannot both be defended at once.

7. The Isolated Queen's Pawn (IQP)

Dynamic compensation and long-term weakness of the isolated d-pawn.

8. Good vs Bad Bishops, and Strategic Exchange Sacrifices

Evaluating bishops by function, and when to sacrifice the exchange.

9. Formulating a Plan

A step-by-step framework for turning positional understanding into a plan.
