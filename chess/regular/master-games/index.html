Master Games

This section is a growing collection of annotated games played by grandmasters, chosen to illustrate recurring ideas in the opening, middlegame, and endgame — the kind of ideas discussed elsewhere in the openings, strategy, tactics, and endgame sections. Each game is presented with the full move list and short annotations explaining the key strategic and tactical turning points, rather than deep engine analysis. The aim is to show these ideas at work in real, high-level play.

A work in progress: more games will be added here over time, organised loosely by theme (classic attacking games, model endgames, instructive positional games, and so on).

1. The Power of the Bishop Pair and a King Hunt

Kasparov – Topalov, 1999: a double exchange sacrifice exposes the black king.

2. "The Game of the Century" — A Queen Sacrifice for Domination

Byrne – Fischer, 1956: a 13-year-old Fischer's queen sacrifice for domination.

3. Development, Development, Development — The Opera Game

Morphy – Duke of Brunswick & Count Isouard, 1858: development over material in the Opera Game.
