1. The Power of the Bishop Pair and a King Hunt

Garry Kasparov – Veselin Topalov

Hoogovens (Wijk aan Zee), 1999 · Pirc Defence

Famous as "Kasparov's Immortal," this game is a model of how a temporary material sacrifice can be used to expose an enemy king that has castled into apparent safety. White's pieces work together with remarkable coordination once the position opens up.

1. e4 d6 2. d4 Nf6 3. Nc3 g6 4. Be3 Bg7 5. Qd2 c6 6. f3 b5 7. Nge2 Nbd7 8. Bh6 Bxh6 9. Qxh6 Bb7 10. a3 e5 11. O-O-O Qe7 12. Kb1 a6 13. Nc1 O-O-O 14. Nb3 exd4 15. Rxd4 c5 16. Rd1 Nb6 17. g3 Kb8 18. Na5 Ba8 19. Bh3 d5 20. Qf4+ Ka7 21. Rhe1 d4 22. Nd5 Nbxd5 23. exd5 Qd6 24. Rxd4! cxd4 25. Re7+ Kb6 26. Qxd4+ Kxa5 27. b4+ Ka4 28. Qc3 Qxd5 29. Ra7 Bb7 30. Rxb7 Qc4 31. Qxf6 Kxa3 32. Qxa6+ Kxb4 33. c3+ Kxc3 34. Qa1+ Kd2 35. Qb2+ Kd1 36. Bf1 Rd2 37. Rd7 Rxd7 38. Bxc4 bxc4 39. Qxh8 Rd3 40. Qa8 c3 41. Qa4+ Ke1 42. f4 f5 43. Kc1 Rd2 44. Qa7 1–0
Key idea (15. Rxd4!): White sacrifices the exchange to rip open the centre and the a-file, transforming a static material edge into a permanent attack on Black's king.
Key idea (24. Rxd4+): A second exchange sacrifice removes the last defender of the black king's shelter, after which White's queen and bishop pair simply overwhelm the king as it is driven across the board.
Takeaway: When the opposing king is exposed, material can often be a secondary consideration — tempo and king safety dominate the evaluation.
