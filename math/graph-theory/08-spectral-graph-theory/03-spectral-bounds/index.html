8.3 Spectral Bounds

The eigenvalues of the adjacency matrix and Laplacian give quantitative bounds on combinatorial parameters like the chromatic number, independence number, and edge expansion.

Chromatic and Independence Number Bounds

Theorem 8.3.1 - Hoffman Bound on Chromatic Number

For any graph $G$ with adjacency eigenvalues $\lambda_1 \ge \cdots \ge \lambda_n$, $$\chi(G) \ge 1 + \frac{\lambda_1}{-\lambda_n}.$$ The bound is tight for complete graphs and strongly regular graphs. It is derived from the observation that an independent set $I$ satisfies $\lambda_1 |I|/n \le |I|(-\lambda_n)/(n - |I|)$ via the Rayleigh quotient.

Theorem 8.3.2 - Delsarte-Hoffman Bound on Independence Number

For a $d$-regular graph on $n$ vertices, $$\alpha(G) \le \frac{n(-\lambda_n)}{\lambda_1 - \lambda_n} = \frac{n(-\lambda_n)}{d - \lambda_n}.$$ The bound follows from the same Rayleigh quotient argument applied to the characteristic vector of an independent set.

Expander Mixing Lemma

Theorem 8.3.3 - Expander Mixing Lemma

Let $G$ be a $d$-regular graph on $n$ vertices with second-largest eigenvalue (in absolute value) $\lambda = \max(|\lambda_2|, |\lambda_n|)$. For any two vertex subsets $S, T \subseteq V$, $$\left| e(S,T) - \frac{d|S||T|}{n} \right| \le \lambda \sqrt{|S||T|},$$ where $e(S,T)$ counts edges between $S$ and $T$. This shows that if $\lambda \ll d$, the graph is pseudo-random: edge distribution closely mimics that of a random $d$-regular graph.

Graphs with small $\lambda/d$ are called expanders; they are crucial in theoretical computer science, coding theory, and derandomisation.

Cheeger Inequality

Definition 8.3.4 - Edge Expansion

The edge expansion (Cheeger constant) of a $d$-regular graph is $$h(G) = \min_{\substack{S \subseteq V \\ 0 < |S| \le n/2}} \frac{e(S, V \setminus S)}{d|S|}.$$ Large $h$ means every small set has many edges leaving it - the graph is well-connected.

Theorem 8.3.5 - Discrete Cheeger Inequality

For a $d$-regular graph with Laplacian second eigenvalue $\mu_2$, $$\frac{h(G)^2}{2d} \le \frac{\mu_2}{d} \le 2h(G).$$ The right inequality (easy direction) shows good expansion implies large $\mu_2$; the left inequality (hard direction, proved via the Fiedler vector) shows large $\mu_2$ implies good expansion.

Ramanujan Graphs

Definition 8.3.6 - Ramanujan Graph

A $d$-regular graph $G$ is a Ramanujan graph if every eigenvalue $\lambda$ of $A$ with $|\lambda| \neq d$ satisfies $|\lambda| \le 2\sqrt{d-1}$. The bound $2\sqrt{d-1}$ is optimal by the Alon-Boppana theorem (asymptotically, no family of $d$-regular graphs can do better). Ramanujan graphs are optimal expanders. Explicit constructions exist (Lubotzky-Phillips-Sarnak, 1988; Margulis, 1988) using deep number theory.
