6.2 Conditional Distributions

A conditional distribution is a version of conditional probability that acts as a full probability measure for almost every conditioning value. The existence of such "regular" conditional distributions relies on topological assumptions on the state space.

Definition 6.2.1 — Regular Conditional Distribution

Let $X$ be a random variable on $(\Omega,\mathcal{F},P)$ and $\mathcal{G} \subseteq \mathcal{F}$ a sub-$\sigma$-algebra. A regular conditional distribution (RCD) of $X$ given $\mathcal{G}$ is a mapping $\mu: \Omega \times \mathcal{B}(\mathbb{R}) \to [0,1]$ such that:

  1. For each $B \in \mathcal{B}(\mathbb{R})$, $\omega \mapsto \mu(\omega, B)$ is $\mathcal{G}$-measurable and equals $P(X \in B \mid \mathcal{G})(\omega)$ a.s.
  2. For $P$-a.e. $\omega$, $B \mapsto \mu(\omega, B)$ is a probability measure on $\mathcal{B}(\mathbb{R})$.

We write $\mu(\omega, \cdot) = P(X \in \cdot \mid \mathcal{G})(\omega)$ and call it the conditional law of $X$ given $\mathcal{G}$ at $\omega$.

Theorem 6.2.2 — Existence for Polish Spaces

If $X$ takes values in a Polish space (complete separable metric space) $S$, then a regular conditional distribution of $X$ given $\mathcal{G}$ always exists. In particular, RCDs exist when $S = \mathbb{R}^d$, $S = C([0,1])$, or any Borel subset of a complete separable metric space.

Conditional Densities

Proposition 6.2.3 — Conditional Density Formula

Suppose $(X, Y)$ has joint density $f_{X,Y}(x,y)$ with respect to Lebesgue measure on $\mathbb{R}^2$, and let $f_X(x) = \int f_{X,Y}(x,y)\,dy > 0$. Then the conditional density of $Y$ given $X = x$ is $$f_{Y|X}(y \mid x) = \frac{f_{X,Y}(x,y)}{f_X(x)},$$ and $E[g(Y) \mid X = x] = \int g(y)\,f_{Y|X}(y\mid x)\,dy$.

This is a concrete representation of the RCD: $\mu(\omega, \cdot)$ at $\omega$ with $X(\omega)=x$ is the measure with density $f_{Y|X}(\cdot\mid x)$.

Bayes' Formula

Theorem 6.2.4 — Bayes' Theorem (Continuous)

Let $X$ (observable) and $\Theta$ (parameter) have joint density $f_{X,\Theta}(x,\theta) = f_{X|\Theta}(x\mid\theta)\,\pi(\theta)$ where $\pi$ is the prior density of $\Theta$. Then the posterior density of $\Theta$ given $X = x$ is $$\pi(\theta \mid x) = f_{\Theta|X}(\theta \mid x) = \frac{f_{X|\Theta}(x\mid\theta)\,\pi(\theta)}{\int f_{X|\Theta}(x\mid\theta')\,\pi(\theta')\,d\theta'} \propto f_{X|\Theta}(x\mid\theta)\,\pi(\theta).$$

This is Bayesian updating: $\text{posterior} \propto \text{likelihood} \times \text{prior}$. It follows directly from the conditional density formula applied to the pair $(\Theta, X)$.

Example — Normal-Normal Conjugacy

Let $\Theta \sim N(\mu_0, \tau^2)$ (prior) and $X \mid \Theta = \theta \sim N(\theta, \sigma^2)$. The posterior is $$\Theta \mid X = x \sim N\!\left(\frac{\sigma^{-2}x + \tau^{-2}\mu_0}{\sigma^{-2}+\tau^{-2}},\; \frac{1}{\sigma^{-2}+\tau^{-2}}\right).$$ The posterior mean is a precision-weighted average of the observation $x$ and the prior mean $\mu_0$. As $\tau^2 \to \infty$ (flat prior), the posterior mean approaches $x$.

Disintegration of Measures

Theorem 6.2.5 — Disintegration

Let $\mu$ be a probability measure on a Polish space $S$ and $f: S \to T$ a measurable map to another Polish space $T$. Let $\nu = f_*\mu$ be the pushforward. Then there exists a family of probability measures $\{\mu_t\}_{t \in T}$ on $S$, the disintegration of $\mu$ along $f$, such that:

  1. $\mu_t$ is supported on $f^{-1}(t)$ for $\nu$-a.e. $t$;
  2. For any measurable $g: S \to [0,\infty)$, $\displaystyle\int_S g\,d\mu = \int_T \left(\int_{f^{-1}(t)} g\,d\mu_t\right)d\nu(t)$.

Taking $f = X$ and $\mu = P$, this is exactly the existence of the RCD: $\mu_x = P(\cdot \mid X = x)$.
