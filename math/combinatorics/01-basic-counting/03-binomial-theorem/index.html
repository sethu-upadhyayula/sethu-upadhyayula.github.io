1.3 Binomial Theorem

The Binomial Theorem gives the expansion of $(x+y)^n$ as an explicit sum. It connects algebra to combinatorics: the coefficients $\binom{n}{k}$ count the number of ways to assign $x$'s and $y$'s from the $n$ factors.

Theorem — Binomial Theorem

For any $n \in \mathbb{N}_0$ and any $x, y$: $$(x + y)^n = \sum_{k=0}^{n} \binom{n}{k} x^k y^{n-k}.$$

Proof

Expand $(x+y)^n = (x+y)(x+y)\cdots(x+y)$ ($n$ factors). Each term in the expansion is obtained by choosing either $x$ or $y$ from each factor. A term $x^k y^{n-k}$ arises when $x$ is chosen from exactly $k$ of the $n$ factors. The number of ways to choose which $k$ factors contribute $x$ is $\binom{n}{k}$. Summing over $k$ gives the theorem. $\square$

Corollary — Sum of Binomial Coefficients

Setting $x = y = 1$: $$\sum_{k=0}^{n} \binom{n}{k} = 2^n.$$ Setting $x = 1, y = -1$: $$\sum_{k=0}^{n} (-1)^k \binom{n}{k} = 0, \quad n \ge 1.$$ These say: the number of subsets equals $2^n$; and the number of even-sized subsets equals the number of odd-sized subsets.

Theorem — Vandermonde's Identity

For non-negative integers $m, n, r$: $$\sum_{k=0}^{r} \binom{m}{k}\binom{n}{r-k} = \binom{m+n}{r}.$$

Proof

Compare coefficients of $x^r$ on both sides of $(1+x)^m (1+x)^n = (1+x)^{m+n}$. The left side gives $\sum_k \binom{m}{k}\binom{n}{r-k}$ (by convolution of coefficients), and the right side gives $\binom{m+n}{r}$. $\square$

Corollary — Sum of Squares of Binomial Coefficients

Setting $m = n$ and $r = n$ in Vandermonde's identity (using $\binom{n}{k} = \binom{n}{n-k}$): $$\sum_{k=0}^{n} \binom{n}{k}^2 = \binom{2n}{n}.$$

Theorem — Multinomial Theorem

For $r$ variables and $n \in \mathbb{N}_0$: $$(x_1 + x_2 + \cdots + x_r)^n = \sum_{\substack{k_1,\ldots,k_r \ge 0 \\ k_1+\cdots+k_r=n}} \binom{n}{k_1, k_2, \ldots, k_r}\, x_1^{k_1} x_2^{k_2} \cdots x_r^{k_r}.$$

Example — Coefficient Extraction

What is the coefficient of $x^3 y^2$ in $(x+y)^5$?

By the binomial theorem with $k=3$: $\binom{5}{3} = 10$.

Example — Multinomial Coefficient

What is the coefficient of $x^2 y^2 z^1$ in $(x+y+z)^5$?

$\binom{5}{2,2,1} = \dfrac{5!}{2!\,2!\,1!} = 30$.
