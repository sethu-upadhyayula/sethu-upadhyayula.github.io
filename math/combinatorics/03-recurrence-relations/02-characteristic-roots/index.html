3.2 Characteristic Roots

The characteristic root method solves homogeneous linear recurrences by converting them to polynomial equations. The roots of the characteristic polynomial determine the form of the general solution.

Definition — Characteristic Polynomial

For the recurrence $a_n = c_1 a_{n-1} + \cdots + c_k a_{n-k}$, the characteristic polynomial is: $$p(r) = r^k - c_1 r^{k-1} - c_2 r^{k-2} - \cdots - c_k.$$ Its roots $r_1, \ldots, r_k$ (counting multiplicity) are the characteristic roots.

Theorem — General Solution (Distinct Roots)

If the characteristic polynomial has $k$ distinct roots $r_1, \ldots, r_k$, then the general solution of the recurrence is: $$a_n = \alpha_1 r_1^n + \alpha_2 r_2^n + \cdots + \alpha_k r_k^n,$$ where $\alpha_1, \ldots, \alpha_k$ are determined by the $k$ initial conditions.

Theorem — General Solution (Repeated Roots)

If $r$ is a root of multiplicity $m$, it contributes terms: $$(A_0 + A_1 n + A_2 n^2 + \cdots + A_{m-1} n^{m-1})\, r^n$$ to the general solution, with $m$ free constants.

Example — Fibonacci (Binet's Formula)

The recurrence $F_n = F_{n-1} + F_{n-2}$ has characteristic polynomial $r^2 - r - 1 = 0$ with roots: $$\phi = \frac{1+\sqrt{5}}{2}, \quad \hat\phi = \frac{1-\sqrt{5}}{2}.$$ General solution: $F_n = \alpha\phi^n + \beta\hat\phi^n$. Using $F_0=0$, $F_1=1$: $$\alpha + \beta = 0, \quad \alpha\phi + \beta\hat\phi = 1 \implies \alpha = \frac{1}{\sqrt5}, \;\beta = -\frac{1}{\sqrt5}.$$ Binet's formula: $$F_n = \frac{\phi^n - \hat\phi^n}{\sqrt{5}}.$$

Example — Repeated Root

The recurrence $a_n = 2a_{n-1} - a_{n-2}$ has characteristic polynomial $(r-1)^2 = 0$ with double root $r=1$. General solution: $a_n = (A + Bn) \cdot 1^n = A + Bn$. With $a_0=1, a_1=3$: $A=1, B=2$, so $a_n = 1+2n$.

Remark — Complex Roots

If the characteristic polynomial has complex roots $r = \rho e^{i\theta}$ and $\bar{r} = \rho e^{-i\theta}$ (conjugate pair), they contribute terms $\rho^n(A\cos(n\theta) + B\sin(n\theta))$, producing oscillating solutions. The magnitude grows like $\rho^n$.
