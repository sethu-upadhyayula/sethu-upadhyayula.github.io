2. Generating Functions

Generating functions encode sequences as formal power series, converting combinatorial identities and recurrence relations into algebraic operations. Herbert Wilf called them "a clothesline on which we hang up a sequence of numbers for display."

Chapter Overview

This chapter develops two types of generating functions:

  • 2.1 Ordinary Generating Functions (OGFs): $A(x) = \sum a_n x^n$; used for unlabelled combinatorial objects and integer partitions.
  • 2.2 Exponential Generating Functions (EGFs): $\hat{A}(x) = \sum a_n x^n/n!$; used for labelled structures such as permutations and set partitions.
  • 2.3 Applications: Catalan numbers, Bell numbers, Stirling numbers, and symbolic methods.

Why Generating Functions?

Generating functions transform recurrence relations into polynomial or rational equations, which are easier to solve. They also encode combinatorial structure: the product of two generating functions corresponds to a "disjoint union" or "Cartesian product" construction on the underlying objects.

Motivating Example

The number of ways to make change for $n$ cents using pennies (1¢) and nickels (5¢) is the coefficient of $x^n$ in: $$\frac{1}{1-x} \cdot \frac{1}{1-x^5}.$$ This product of OGFs reflects the independence of choosing how many pennies and how many nickels to use.
