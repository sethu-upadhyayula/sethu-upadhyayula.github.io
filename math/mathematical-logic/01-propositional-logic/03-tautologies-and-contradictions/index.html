1.3 Tautologies and Contradictions

Definition — Tautology

A formula $\phi$ is a tautology if it is true under every possible truth-value assignment to its variables. We write $\models \phi$ (or $\vDash \phi$) to mean $\phi$ is a tautology.

Definition — Contradiction

A formula $\phi$ is a contradiction (or unsatisfiable) if it is false under every truth-value assignment. Equivalently, $\neg\phi$ is a tautology.

Definition — Contingency

A formula that is neither a tautology nor a contradiction is called a contingency. Its truth value genuinely depends on the assignment.

Examples
  • Tautologies: $P \vee \neg P$ (law of excluded middle), $P \Rightarrow P$, $(P \Rightarrow Q) \Leftrightarrow (\neg Q \Rightarrow \neg P)$.
  • Contradictions: $P \wedge \neg P$, $(P \Rightarrow Q) \wedge P \wedge \neg Q$.
  • Contingencies: $P$, $P \wedge Q$, $P \Rightarrow Q$.

Logical Equivalence via Tautology

Theorem

$A \equiv B$ if and only if $A \Leftrightarrow B$ is a tautology.

Key Logical Equivalences

Catalogue of Equivalences

The following equivalences hold for all propositions $P$, $Q$, $R$:

  • Double Negation: $\neg\neg P \equiv P$.
  • Idempotency: $P \wedge P \equiv P$; $P \vee P \equiv P$.
  • Commutativity: $P \wedge Q \equiv Q \wedge P$; $P \vee Q \equiv Q \vee P$.
  • Associativity: $(P \wedge Q) \wedge R \equiv P \wedge (Q \wedge R)$; similarly for $\vee$.
  • Distribution: $P \wedge (Q \vee R) \equiv (P \wedge Q) \vee (P \wedge R)$; $P \vee (Q \wedge R) \equiv (P \vee Q) \wedge (P \vee R)$.
  • Absorption: $P \wedge (P \vee Q) \equiv P$; $P \vee (P \wedge Q) \equiv P$.
  • De Morgan: $\neg(P \wedge Q) \equiv \neg P \vee \neg Q$; $\neg(P \vee Q) \equiv \neg P \wedge \neg Q$.
  • Implication: $P \Rightarrow Q \equiv \neg P \vee Q \equiv \neg Q \Rightarrow \neg P$.
  • Exportation: $(P \wedge Q) \Rightarrow R \equiv P \Rightarrow (Q \Rightarrow R)$.

Each can be verified by constructing a truth table.
