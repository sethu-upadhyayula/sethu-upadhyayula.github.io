1.4 Normal Forms

Definition — Literal

A literal is a propositional variable $P$ or its negation $\neg P$. Literals are the building blocks of normal forms.

Definition — CNF and DNF
  • A clause is a disjunction of literals: $l_1 \vee l_2 \vee \cdots \vee l_k$.
  • A formula is in Conjunctive Normal Form (CNF) if it is a conjunction of clauses: $(C_1) \wedge (C_2) \wedge \cdots \wedge (C_m)$.
  • A term (minterm) is a conjunction of literals: $l_1 \wedge l_2 \wedge \cdots \wedge l_k$.
  • A formula is in Disjunctive Normal Form (DNF) if it is a disjunction of terms: $(T_1) \vee (T_2) \vee \cdots \vee (T_m)$.

Theorem: Every propositional formula is logically equivalent to some formula in CNF and to some formula in DNF.

Conversion via Truth Table

Example — Truth Table to DNF and CNF

Consider $\phi = P \Rightarrow Q$ (equivalently $\neg P \vee Q$). Its truth table:

$P$$Q$$\phi$
TTT
TFF
FTT
FFT

DNF — take rows where $\phi = T$ (rows 1, 3, 4) and form minterms: $$(P \wedge Q) \vee (\neg P \wedge Q) \vee (\neg P \wedge \neg Q).$$

CNF — take rows where $\phi = F$ (row 2) and form maxterm (negate the minterm of the false row): $$(\neg P \vee Q).$$

Both are equivalent to $\neg P \vee Q$, confirming $P \Rightarrow Q \equiv \neg P \vee Q$.

Resolution

Resolution Rule

Given two clauses containing complementary literals $x$ and $\neg x$: $$\frac{C_1 \vee x \qquad C_2 \vee \neg x}{C_1 \vee C_2}$$ The resolvent $C_1 \vee C_2$ is a logical consequence of the two parent clauses.

Resolution is sound: the resolvent is always a semantic consequence of its parents.

Theorem — Completeness of Resolution for Refutation

A set $S$ of clauses is unsatisfiable (contradictory) if and only if the empty clause $\bot$ can be derived from $S$ by repeated application of the resolution rule. This makes resolution a decision procedure for propositional unsatisfiability.

Example — Resolution Refutation

Show $\{P \vee Q,\; \neg P \vee Q,\; \neg Q\}$ is unsatisfiable.

  1. Resolve $P \vee Q$ and $\neg P \vee Q$ on $P$: get $Q$.
  2. Resolve $Q$ and $\neg Q$: get $\bot$.

The empty clause is derived, so the set is unsatisfiable.
