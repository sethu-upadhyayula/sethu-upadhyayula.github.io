4.3 Geometric Brownian Motion

Geometric Brownian motion (GBM) is the simplest SDE in which the diffusion coefficient is proportional to the current value of the process. It ensures positivity and gives log-normally distributed increments, making it the foundation of the Black-Scholes model.

Definition 4.3.1 — Geometric Brownian Motion

A process $S_t$ is a geometric Brownian motion with drift $\mu \in \mathbb{R}$ and volatility $\sigma > 0$ if it satisfies

$$dS_t = \mu S_t\,dt + \sigma S_t\,dW_t, \quad S_0 = S_0 > 0.$$ This is the homogeneous linear SDE with $a = \mu$, $c = \sigma$, $b = d = 0$.
Theorem 4.3.2 — Explicit Solution of GBM

The unique strong solution is

$$S_t = S_0 \exp\!\left(\left(\mu - \frac{\sigma^2}{2}\right)t + \sigma W_t\right).$$

In particular, $S_t > 0$ for all $t \geq 0$ almost surely.

Proof

Apply Itô's formula to $f(t,x) = \log x$ with $dS = \mu S\,dt + \sigma S\,dW$:

$$d(\log S_t) = \frac{1}{S_t}\,dS_t - \frac{1}{2S_t^2}(dS_t)^2 = \frac{\mu S_t\,dt + \sigma S_t\,dW_t}{S_t} - \frac{\sigma^2 S_t^2\,dt}{2S_t^2}$$ $$= \mu\,dt + \sigma\,dW_t - \frac{\sigma^2}{2}\,dt = \left(\mu - \frac{\sigma^2}{2}\right)dt + \sigma\,dW_t.$$ Integrating: $\log(S_t/S_0) = (\mu - \sigma^2/2)t + \sigma W_t$. Exponentiating gives the result. $\square$

Log-Normal Distribution

Proposition 4.3.3 — Distribution of $S_t$

Since $W_t \sim \mathcal{N}(0,t)$, we have $\log(S_t/S_0) \sim \mathcal{N}((\mu-\sigma^2/2)t,\, \sigma^2 t)$. Therefore $S_t$ has a log-normal distribution:

$$S_t \sim \mathrm{LogNormal}\!\left(\log S_0 + \left(\mu - \tfrac{\sigma^2}{2}\right)t,\; \sigma^2 t\right).$$ The moments are: $$\mathbb{E}[S_t] = S_0 e^{\mu t}, \qquad \mathrm{Var}(S_t) = S_0^2 e^{2\mu t}\bigl(e^{\sigma^2 t} - 1\bigr).$$
Proof of $\mathbb{E}[S_t] = S_0 e^{\mu t}$

Using the moment generating function of a normal: $\mathbb{E}[e^{\sigma W_t}] = e^{\sigma^2 t/2}$, so

$$\mathbb{E}[S_t] = S_0 e^{(\mu-\sigma^2/2)t}\mathbb{E}[e^{\sigma W_t}] = S_0 e^{(\mu-\sigma^2/2)t} \cdot e^{\sigma^2 t/2} = S_0 e^{\mu t}. \quad \square$$
Remark 4.3.4 — Itô Drift vs. Geometric Growth

The parameter $\mu$ in the SDE is the instantaneous expected return: $\mathbb{E}[dS]/S = \mu\,dt$. However, the median and most probable growth rate are $(\mu - \sigma^2/2)t$, which is less. The gap $\sigma^2/2$ is the Itô correction. In the Black-Scholes model, the stock price $S_t$ under the physical measure satisfies GBM, and under the risk-neutral measure, $\mu$ is replaced by the risk-free rate $r$.

Relevance to Black-Scholes

Example 4.3.5 — Black-Scholes Pricing

Under the risk-neutral measure $\mathbb{Q}$, the stock price satisfies $dS = rS\,dt + \sigma S\,d\tilde{W}_t$ where $\tilde{W}_t$ is a $\mathbb{Q}$-Brownian motion. The price of a European call option with strike $K$ and maturity $T$ is

$$C(t, S) = e^{-r(T-t)}\mathbb{E}^{\mathbb{Q}}\!\left[(S_T - K)^+\,\big|\,S_t = S\right].$$ Substituting $S_T = S e^{(r-\sigma^2/2)(T-t)+\sigma\tilde{W}_{T-t}}$ and computing the expectation yields the Black-Scholes formula: $$C = S\Phi(d_+) - Ke^{-r(T-t)}\Phi(d_-),$$ where $d_{\pm} = \frac{\log(S/K) + (r \pm \sigma^2/2)(T-t)}{\sigma\sqrt{T-t}}$ and $\Phi$ is the standard normal CDF.
Corollary 4.3.6 — Itô SDE for the Log-Price

Let $Y_t = \log S_t$. Then $Y_t$ satisfies the linear (additive-noise) SDE

$$dY_t = \left(\mu - \frac{\sigma^2}{2}\right)dt + \sigma\,dW_t,$$ which is arithmetic Brownian motion with constant drift and volatility. Simulation and estimation of GBM is therefore equivalent to working with the log-price process.
