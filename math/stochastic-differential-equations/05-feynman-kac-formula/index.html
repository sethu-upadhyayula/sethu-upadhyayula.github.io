Chapter 5: The Feynman-Kac Formula

The Feynman-Kac formula establishes a profound connection between stochastic processes and partial differential equations: the expected value of a functional of a diffusion solves a parabolic or elliptic PDE. Originally used by Richard Feynman in the context of quantum mechanics and formalised by Mark Kac, this duality is central to mathematical finance, computational physics, and the theory of PDEs.

Chapter Overview

5.1 - Connections to PDEs

Given a diffusion $dX = b\,dt + \sigma\,dW$, its generator $\mathcal{L}u = b\cdot\nabla u + \frac{1}{2}\sigma\sigma^\top:\nabla^2 u$ governs the evolution of expected values. The Kolmogorov backward equation and the Fokker-Planck (forward) equation provide a complete probabilistic picture: the former governs expectations, the latter the density of the process.

5.2 - Feynman-Kac Formula

The full Feynman-Kac theorem states that $u(t,x) = E\!\left[e^{-\int_t^T c(X_s)\,ds}g(X_T) + \int_t^T e^{-\int_t^s c\,dr}f(X_s)\,ds\,\Big|\,X_t = x\right]$ solves the parabolic PDE $\partial_t u + \mathcal{L}u - cu + f = 0$ with terminal condition $u(T,\cdot) = g$. Applications to the Black-Scholes PDE and to Monte Carlo option pricing are developed.
