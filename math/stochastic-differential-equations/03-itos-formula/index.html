Chapter 3: Itô's Formula

Itô's formula is the stochastic analogue of the chain rule. If $X_t$ is a semimartingale and $f$ is a smooth function, the formula tells us how to compute $df(t, X_t)$. The crucial difference from classical calculus is the appearance of a second-order correction term involving the quadratic variation of $X$, which arises because $dW_t^2 = dt \ne 0$.

This chapter covers the one-dimensional formula, its extension to multiple dimensions, and key applications including the log transformation of geometric Brownian motion, the product rule, and a preview of the Black-Scholes PDE.

Chapter Overview

3.1 - One-Dimensional Itô Formula

For $dX = \mu\,dt + \sigma\,dW$ and $f \in C^{1,2}$: $df(t,X_t) = f_t\,dt + f_x\,dX + \frac{1}{2}f_{xx}\sigma^2\,dt$. We derive this via Taylor expansion and the multiplication table $dW^2 = dt$.

3.2 - Multi-Dimensional Itô Formula

For a vector process $X = (X^1,\ldots,X^n)$ and $f \in C^{1,2}$: $df = f_t\,dt + \sum_i f_{x_i}\,dX^i + \frac{1}{2}\sum_{i,j} f_{x_ix_j}\,d[X^i,X^j]$, where $d[W^i,W^j] = \rho_{ij}\,dt$ encodes correlations between driving Brownian motions.

3.3 - Applications

We apply Itô's formula to: derive the log-normal dynamics of GBM, compute $d(e^{-rt}V)$ for pricing, show that $e^{\theta W_t - \theta^2 t/2}$ is a martingale (Doléans-Dade exponential), and sketch the derivation of the Black-Scholes PDE.
