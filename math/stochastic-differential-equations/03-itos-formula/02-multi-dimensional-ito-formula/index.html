3.2 Multi-Dimensional Itô Formula

Many applications - including multi-asset option pricing and the derivation of PDEs from SDEs - require a version of Itô's formula for vector-valued processes driven by multiple Brownian motions.

Setup - Multi-Dimensional Itô Processes

Let $W = (W^1, \ldots, W^m)$ be an $m$-dimensional Brownian motion on $(\Omega, \mathcal{F}, P)$, meaning each $W^j$ is a standard Brownian motion and $[W^i, W^j]_t = \rho_{ij} t$ for a correlation matrix $(\rho_{ij})$ with $\rho_{ii} = 1$.

Let $X = (X^1, \ldots, X^n)$ be an $n$-dimensional Itô process: $dX^i_t = \mu^i_t\,dt + \sum_{j=1}^m \sigma^{ij}_t\,dW^j_t$ for $i = 1, \ldots, n$.

Theorem 3.2.1 - Multi-Dimensional Itô Formula

Let $f(t, x) \in C^{1,2}([0,T] \times \mathbb{R}^n)$. Then:

$$df(t, X_t) = \frac{\partial f}{\partial t}\,dt + \sum_{i=1}^n \frac{\partial f}{\partial x_i}\,dX^i_t + \frac{1}{2}\sum_{i,j=1}^n \frac{\partial^2 f}{\partial x_i \partial x_j}\,d[X^i, X^j]_t.$$

The cross-quadratic-variation is:

$$d[X^i, X^j]_t = \left(\sum_{k=1}^m \sigma^{ik}_t \sigma^{jk}_t\right)dt =: ((\Sigma \Sigma^\top)_{ij})\,dt$$

where $\Sigma = (\sigma^{ij})$ is the volatility matrix. Equivalently, $d[X^i,X^j] = \sum_k \sigma^{ik}\sigma^{jk}\,dt$, which is the $(i,j)$ entry of the covariance matrix $\Sigma\Sigma^\top$.

Remark - The Itô Multiplication Table in $n$ Dimensions

The rules are: $dW^i \cdot dW^j = \rho_{ij}\,dt$, $dW^i \cdot dt = 0$, $dt \cdot dt = 0$. The correlation $\rho_{ij}$ between the $i$-th and $j$-th Brownian motions enters directly into the Itô correction term.

For independent Brownian motions ($\rho_{ij} = \delta_{ij}$), the off-diagonal cross-quadratic variations vanish: $d[W^i, W^j] = 0$ for $i \ne j$.

Example 3.2.2 - Two Correlated Assets

Let $S^1_t$ and $S^2_t$ follow: $dS^k = \mu^k S^k\,dt + \sigma^k S^k\,dW^k$ where $d[W^1, W^2]_t = \rho\,dt$. Apply the formula to $f(S^1, S^2) = \ln(S^1 S^2) = \ln S^1 + \ln S^2$:

$$d\ln(S^1 S^2) = d\ln S^1 + d\ln S^2 = \left(\mu^1 - \frac{(\sigma^1)^2}{2}\right)dt + \sigma^1\,dW^1 + \left(\mu^2 - \frac{(\sigma^2)^2}{2}\right)dt + \sigma^2\,dW^2.$$

Now consider $f(S^1, S^2) = S^1 S^2$. By the product rule: $d(S^1 S^2) = S^2\,dS^1 + S^1\,dS^2 + d[S^1,S^2]$ where $d[S^1,S^2] = \sigma^1\sigma^2\rho S^1 S^2\,dt$, giving drift $(\mu^1 + \mu^2 + \rho\sigma^1\sigma^2)S^1S^2\,dt$.
