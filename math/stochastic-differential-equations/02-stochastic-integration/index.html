Chapter 2: Stochastic Integration

The Itô integral extends the classical Riemann-Stieltjes integral to integrators with infinite variation - specifically Brownian motion. Because Brownian paths have unbounded variation on every interval, the classical construction fails, and a new approach based on $L^2$ approximation and the isometry property is needed.

The key insight is to use left-endpoint Riemann sums, which ensures that the integrand at each step uses only past information, making the integral a martingale. This is in contrast to the Stratonovich integral, which uses midpoints and obeys the classical chain rule.

Chapter Overview

2.1 - Itô Integral

We define $\int_0^t f_s\,dW_s$ first for simple adapted processes as a weighted sum of Brownian increments with left-endpoint evaluation, then extend to general $f \in \mathcal{L}^2$ by $L^2$ limits. The result is a continuous martingale.

2.2 - Properties of the Itô Integral

The integral is linear, has zero mean, and its quadratic variation equals $\int_0^t f_s^2\,ds$. We compare the Itô and Stratonovich conventions and explain when each is preferred.

2.3 - Itô Isometry

The Itô isometry $E\!\left[(\int_0^T f_s\,dW_s)^2\right] = E\!\left[\int_0^T f_s^2\,ds\right]$ is the key tool that allows the integral to be extended from simple to general integrands. We prove it and state the generalisation to cross-products.
