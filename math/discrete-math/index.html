Discrete Math

Discrete mathematics studies structures that are fundamentally discrete rather than continuous. The five sections below cover abstract algebra, number theory, combinatorics, graph theory, and algorithm design — material with applications across computer science, cryptography, and pure mathematics.

Abstract Algebra

Groups, rings, fields, and the algebraic structures underlying much of modern mathematics.

Number Theory

Divisibility, primes, modular arithmetic, and the properties of the integers.

Combinatorics

Counting principles, permutations and combinations, generating functions, and combinatorial structures.

Graph Theory

Graphs, trees, networks, connectivity, and algorithms on discrete structures.

Algorithm Design

Algorithmic paradigms, complexity analysis, and techniques for designing efficient algorithms.
