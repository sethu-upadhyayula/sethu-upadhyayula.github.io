3. Connectedness

Connectedness captures the idea that a space is "in one piece." The formal definition avoids any notion of distance, relying purely on the open set structure. There are several related notions — connectivity, path-connectivity, and local variants — each relevant in different contexts.

The Intermediate Value Theorem of classical analysis is a direct consequence of the connectedness of closed intervals in $\mathbb{R}$, illustrating how topological reasoning subsumes analytic results.
