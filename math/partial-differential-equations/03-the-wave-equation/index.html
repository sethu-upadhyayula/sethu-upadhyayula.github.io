Chapter 3: The Wave Equation

The wave equation $u_{tt} = c^2 u_{xx}$ is the prototypical hyperbolic PDE. It governs vibrating strings, acoustic waves, and electromagnetic fields. This chapter develops d'Alembert's elegant closed-form solution, the method of separation of variables for bounded domains, and energy methods for proving uniqueness.

Chapter Overview

3.1 - d'Alembert's Formula

Derivation via the change of variables $\xi = x+ct$, $\eta = x-ct$ reducing $u_{tt}=c^2u_{xx}$ to $u_{\xi\eta}=0$. The explicit formula $u = \frac{\phi(x+ct)+\phi(x-ct)}{2} + \frac{1}{2c}\int_{x-ct}^{x+ct}\psi\,dy$ and domain of dependence.

3.2 - Separation of Variables

Eigenfunctions $X_n = \sin(n\pi x/L)$, eigenvalues $\lambda_n = (n\pi/L)^2$; Fourier series solution; normal modes of vibration.

3.3 - Energy Methods

The energy $E(t) = \frac{1}{2}\int(u_t^2 + c^2 u_x^2)\,dx$ is conserved: $E'(t)=0$. Used to prove uniqueness and stability.
