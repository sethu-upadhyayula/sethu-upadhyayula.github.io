Chapter 1: Descriptive Statistics

Descriptive statistics summarise the essential features of a dataset without making probabilistic inferences. We organise raw data through measures of centre, spread, and shape, and visualise structure using boxplots, histograms, and scatter plots.

Chapter Overview

1.1 — Measures of Location

Mean $\bar{x}$, median, mode, trimmed mean, and weighted mean. The mean minimises $\sum(x_i - c)^2$; the median minimises $\sum|x_i - c|$.

1.2 — Measures of Spread

Range, IQR, sample variance $s^2 = \frac{1}{n-1}\sum(x_i-\bar{x})^2$ (Bessel correction), standard deviation, MAD, coefficient of variation, skewness, and kurtosis.

1.3 — Exploratory Data Analysis

Boxplots, histograms, QQ-plots, scatter plots, and the sample correlation $r = \frac{\sum(x_i-\bar{x})(y_i-\bar{y})}{\sqrt{\sum(x_i-\bar{x})^2\sum(y_i-\bar{y})^2}}$.
