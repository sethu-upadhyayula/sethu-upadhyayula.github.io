Chapter 1: Markov Chains

Markov chains are the most fundamental class of stochastic processes. A discrete-time Markov chain (DTMC) is a sequence of random variables $(X_n)_{n \ge 0}$ taking values in a countable state space $S$, with the defining property that the future is independent of the past given the present. This Markov property reduces the infinite-dimensional problem of describing a random process to the study of a single matrix — the transition matrix.

Despite their simplicity, Markov chains model a remarkable range of phenomena: random walks, queuing systems, population genetics, PageRank, and Markov chain Monte Carlo (MCMC) algorithms all rely on this framework.

Chapter Overview

1.1 — Transition Matrices

We define the Markov property $P(X_{n+1}=j \mid X_0,\ldots,X_n) = P(X_{n+1}=j \mid X_n)$ and introduce the transition matrix $P$ with entries $P_{ij} = P(X_1 = j \mid X_0 = i)$. The $n$-step transition probabilities are given by $P^n$, and the Chapman-Kolmogorov equations $P^{m+n} = P^m P^n$ follow immediately.

1.2 — Classification of States

States communicate if they can reach each other; the chain partitions into communicating classes. The period $d(i) = \gcd\{n : (P^n)_{ii} > 0\}$ measures the cyclicity of a state. A state is recurrent if the chain returns to it with probability 1, and transient otherwise. Recurrent states split into positive recurrent (finite mean return time) and null recurrent.

1.3 — Stationary Distributions

A probability vector $\pi$ satisfying $\pi P = \pi$ is called a stationary distribution. For irreducible positive recurrent chains, $\pi$ exists, is unique, and equals $\pi_i = 1/E_i[T_i]$ where $T_i$ is the first return time to $i$. Detailed balance provides a convenient sufficient condition.

1.4 — Ergodic Theorem

An ergodic chain (irreducible, positive recurrent, aperiodic) satisfies $(P^n)_{ij} \to \pi_j$ as $n \to \infty$. Time averages converge almost surely: $\frac{1}{n}\sum_{k=0}^{n-1} f(X_k) \to \sum_i \pi_i f(i)$. Finite irreducible aperiodic chains converge geometrically fast.
