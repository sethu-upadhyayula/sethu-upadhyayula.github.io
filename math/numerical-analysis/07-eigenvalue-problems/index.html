7. Eigenvalue Problems

Computing eigenvalues and eigenvectors of large matrices is one of the central problems of numerical linear algebra, with applications ranging from structural vibration analysis and quantum mechanics to Google's PageRank and principal component analysis. This chapter presents the three main algorithmic families: the power method and its inverse-iteration variant for targeting individual eigenvalues; the QR algorithm for computing the complete Schur decomposition; and the Golub-Reinsch algorithm for the singular value decomposition.

We pay careful attention to convergence rates, to the practical Hessenberg reduction that makes QR feasible, and to the numerical rank and condition number concepts that arise in the SVD.
