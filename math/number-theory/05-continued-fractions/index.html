Chapter 5: Continued Fractions

Continued fractions are expressions of the form $a_0 + \cfrac{1}{a_1 + \cfrac{1}{a_2 + \cdots}}$ and provide the best rational approximations to real numbers in a precise sense. Every irrational number has a unique infinite continued fraction expansion, and the rational approximations obtained by truncating this expansion — the convergents — are optimal among all fractions with denominators of bounded size.

The theory culminates in a beautiful application: Pell's equation $x^2 - Dy^2 = 1$, whose fundamental solution is always found among the convergents of the continued fraction expansion of $\sqrt{D}$.

Chapter Overview

5.1 — Finite and Infinite Continued Fractions

We define continued fractions $[a_0; a_1, a_2, \ldots]$, show that finite continued fractions represent exactly the rationals (with two representations each), and that infinite continued fractions represent irrationals uniquely. The CF algorithm — repeatedly taking floor and reciprocal — generates the partial quotients $a_i$. Classic examples: $\sqrt{2} = [1; \overline{2}]$, $\phi = [1; \overline{1}]$, $\pi = [3; 7, 15, 1, 292, \ldots]$.

5.2 — Convergents

The convergent $p_n/q_n = [a_0; a_1, \ldots, a_n]$ satisfies the recurrences $p_n = a_n p_{n-1} + p_{n-2}$ and $q_n = a_n q_{n-1} + q_{n-2}$. The key identity $p_n q_{n-1} - p_{n-1} q_n = (-1)^{n-1}$ shows convergents are always in lowest terms and alternate above/below $\alpha$. The best approximation theorem states convergents are the best rational approximants: $|\alpha - p_n/q_n| < 1/(q_n q_{n+1}) \le 1/q_n^2$.

5.3 — Pell's Equation

The equation $x^2 - Dy^2 = 1$ (for non-square $D > 0$) always has infinitely many positive integer solutions. The CF expansion of $\sqrt{D}$ is eventually periodic; the fundamental solution $(x_1, y_1)$ equals the convergent $(p_{k-1}, q_{k-1})$ just before the first full period. All solutions are generated by $(x_n + y_n\sqrt{D}) = (x_1 + y_1\sqrt{D})^n$.
