3.2 Inequality Constraints and KKT Conditions

The Karush-Kuhn-Tucker (KKT) conditions extend Lagrange multiplier theory to handle inequality constraints. They are the fundamental first-order necessary conditions for constrained nonlinear programming and are the starting point for algorithm design, duality theory, and sensitivity analysis.

General Problem and Lagrangian

Definition 3.2.1 - General Constrained Problem

The general nonlinear programme is: $$\min_{x\in\mathbb{R}^n} f(x) \quad\text{s.t.}\quad g_i(x)\le0\;(i=1,\ldots,m),\quad h_j(x)=0\;(j=1,\ldots,p).$$

The Lagrangian is $\mathcal{L}(x,\mu,\lambda)=f(x)+\sum_{i=1}^m\mu_ig_i(x)+\sum_{j=1}^p\lambda_jh_j(x)$, with dual variables $\mu_i\ge0$ (for inequalities) and $\lambda_j\in\mathbb{R}$ (for equalities).

KKT Conditions

Theorem 3.2.2 - KKT Necessary Conditions

If $x^*$ is a local minimum and a constraint qualification holds, then there exist multipliers $\mu^*\in\mathbb{R}^m$ and $\lambda^*\in\mathbb{R}^p$ satisfying the KKT conditions:

  1. Stationarity: $\nabla_x\mathcal{L}(x^*,\mu^*,\lambda^*) = \nabla f(x^*) + \sum_i\mu_i^*\nabla g_i(x^*) + \sum_j\lambda_j^*\nabla h_j(x^*) = 0$.
  2. Primal feasibility: $g_i(x^*)\le0$ for all $i$; $h_j(x^*)=0$ for all $j$.
  3. Dual feasibility: $\mu_i^*\ge0$ for all $i$.
  4. Complementary slackness: $\mu_i^*g_i(x^*)=0$ for all $i$.

Complementary slackness means: for each inequality, either the constraint is active ($g_i(x^*)=0$) or its multiplier is zero ($\mu_i^*=0$), but not both (generically).

Definition 3.2.3 - Active Set

The active set at a feasible point $x$ is $\mathcal{A}(x)=\{i:g_i(x)=0\}$ - the set of inequality constraints that are tight. Active constraints behave locally like equality constraints and generate nonzero multipliers at the optimum.

Sufficiency for Convex Problems

Theorem 3.2.4 - KKT Sufficiency (Convex Programme)

Suppose $f$ and $g_i$ are convex, $h_j$ are affine, and $(x^*,\mu^*,\lambda^*)$ satisfy the KKT conditions. Then $x^*$ is a global minimiser of the convex programme. The converse holds under Slater's condition: any global minimiser has KKT multipliers. This makes the KKT conditions both necessary and sufficient for convex programmes under Slater's condition.

Example - KKT for a Quadratic Programme

Minimise $\frac{1}{2}x^TQx+c^Tx$ subject to $Ax\le b$ with $Q\succ0$. The KKT conditions are: $Qx^*+c+A^T\mu^*=0$, $Ax^*\le b$, $\mu^*\ge0$, $\mu_i^*(A_ix^*-b_i)=0$. This is a linear complementarity problem (LCP). For active set methods, one solves a sequence of equality-constrained QPs with the currently active constraints.

The KKT conditions have a unique solution here because $Q\succ0$ guarantees strict convexity of the objective.
