5.2 Interpolation Error

By Céa's lemma, the FEM error is controlled by the best approximation error $\min_{v_h \in V_h}\|u - v_h\|_V$. To make this concrete we need to bound the error when $u$ is approximated by its nodal interpolant $I_h u \in V_h$. The key tool is the Bramble-Hilbert lemma, which relates the interpolation error to higher Sobolev seminorms of $u$.

The Nodal Interpolation Operator

Definition - Nodal Interpolant

Let $\mathcal{T}_h$ be a triangulation of $\Omega$ and let $V_h$ be the space of continuous piecewise polynomials of degree $k$. The nodal interpolation operator $I_h: C^0(\bar\Omega) \to V_h$ is defined by requiring that $I_h u$ agrees with $u$ at all nodal points $\{x_i\}$: $$I_h u = \sum_i u(x_i)\,\varphi_i,$$ where $\{\varphi_i\}$ are the global Lagrange basis functions satisfying $\varphi_i(x_j) = \delta_{ij}$. On each element $K$, the local interpolant is $$I_h u\big|_K = \sum_{i=1}^{N_{\mathrm{loc}}} u(x_i^K)\,\hat\varphi_i \circ F_K^{-1},$$ where $x_i^K$ are the local nodes on $K$ and $F_K$ is the affine map from the reference element.

The Bramble-Hilbert Lemma

The Bramble-Hilbert lemma is an abstract result that quantifies how well a bounded linear functional (here, the error $u \mapsto u - Pu$) can fail to vanish on polynomials of degree $\leq k$.

Theorem - Bramble-Hilbert Lemma

Let $K \subset \mathbb{R}^d$ be a bounded Lipschitz domain with diameter $h_K$. Let $P: W^{k+1,p}(K) \to \mathcal{P}_k(K)$ be a bounded linear operator that reproduces polynomials of degree $\leq k$, i.e., $Pp = p$ for all $p \in \mathcal{P}_k(K)$. Then there exists a constant $C$ depending only on $k$, $p$, and the shape of $K$ (not on $h_K$) such that $$\|u - Pu\|_{W^{m,p}(K)} \leq C\, h_K^{k+1-m}\, |u|_{W^{k+1,p}(K)} \quad \forall\, u \in W^{k+1,p}(K),$$ for $0 \leq m \leq k+1$, where $|u|_{W^{k+1,p}(K)}$ denotes the $(k+1)$-th order Sobolev seminorm.

Remark - Scaling Argument

The power $h_K^{k+1-m}$ arises from a scaling argument: map $K$ to a reference element $\hat K$ of unit size, apply the estimate on $\hat K$, then scale back. Each spatial derivative contributes a factor of $h_K^{-1}$, and a $(k+1)$-th order seminorm scaled by $h_K^{k+1}$ gives net $h_K^{k+1-m}$ in the $W^{m,p}$ norm.

Interpolation Error on a Single Element

For the nodal interpolant $I_h$ onto $P_k$ elements ($k \geq 1$), the operator $I_h$ reproduces all polynomials of degree $\leq k$. Applying the Bramble-Hilbert lemma directly (using $p=2$ for $L^2$-based Sobolev spaces):

Theorem - Local Interpolation Error ($P_k$ Elements)

Let $K$ be a shape-regular element with diameter $h_K$. For $u \in H^{k+1}(K)$ and $0 \leq m \leq k+1$: $$\|u - I_h u\|_{H^m(K)} \leq C\, h_K^{k+1-m}\, |u|_{H^{k+1}(K)},$$ where $C$ depends on $k$, $d$, and the shape regularity constant of the mesh but not on $h_K$ or $u$. In particular, for $P_1$ elements ($k=1$): $$\|u - I_h u\|_{L^2(K)} \leq C\, h_K^2\, |u|_{H^2(K)}, \qquad \|u - I_h u\|_{H^1(K)} \leq C\, h_K\, |u|_{H^2(K)}.$$

Global Interpolation Error

Summing the local estimates over all elements in the triangulation $\mathcal{T}_h$, with global mesh size $h = \max_{K \in \mathcal{T}_h} h_K$:

Theorem - Global Interpolation Error

Let $\mathcal{T}_h$ be a shape-regular family of triangulations. For $u \in H^{k+1}(\Omega)$ and $0 \leq m \leq k+1$: $$\|u - I_h u\|_{H^m(\Omega)} \leq C\, h^{k+1-m}\, \|u\|_{H^{k+1}(\Omega)}.$$ For $P_1$ elements: $$\|u - I_h u\|_{H^1(\Omega)} \leq C\, h\, \|u\|_{H^2(\Omega)}, \qquad \|u - I_h u\|_{L^2(\Omega)} \leq C\, h^2\, \|u\|_{H^2(\Omega)}.$$

Proof sketch. Square both sides of the local estimate and sum: $$\|u - I_h u\|_{H^m(\Omega)}^2 = \sum_K \|u - I_h u\|_{H^m(K)}^2 \leq C^2 h^{2(k+1-m)} \sum_K |u|_{H^{k+1}(K)}^2 = C^2 h^{2(k+1-m)} |u|_{H^{k+1}(\Omega)}^2.$$ Taking square roots and bounding the seminorm by the full norm gives the result. $\square$

Shape Regularity

Definition - Shape Regularity

A family of triangulations $\{\mathcal{T}_h\}$ is shape-regular if there exists a constant $\sigma > 0$ such that for all $h$ and all $K \in \mathcal{T}_h$: $$\frac{h_K}{\rho_K} \leq \sigma,$$ where $\rho_K$ is the diameter of the largest ball inscribed in $K$. Shape regularity prevents elements from degenerating (becoming flat or needle-like), which would destroy the scaling argument in the Bramble-Hilbert estimate. Under shape regularity, the constant $C$ in the interpolation estimates is uniform across all mesh refinements.
