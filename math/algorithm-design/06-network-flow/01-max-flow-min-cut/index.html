6.1 Max-Flow Min-Cut

The max-flow min-cut theorem is one of the deepest results in combinatorial optimisation. It asserts that the maximum amount of flow that can be routed from source to sink equals the minimum capacity that must be "severed" to disconnect them — a beautiful primal-dual relationship.

Definition 6.1.1 — Flow Network

A flow network is a directed graph $G=(V,E)$ with a capacity function $c: E \to \mathbb{R}_+$, a designated source $s \in V$, and a designated sink $t \in V$. We assume no edge enters $s$ and no edge leaves $t$ (without loss of generality).

A flow is a function $f: E \to \mathbb{R}_{\ge 0}$ satisfying: (1) Capacity: $0 \le f(e) \le c(e)$ for all $e \in E$. (2) Conservation: for every $v \ne s,t$, $\sum_{(u,v)\in E} f(u,v) = \sum_{(v,w)\in E} f(v,w)$.

Definition 6.1.2 — Flow Value and Cut

The value of flow $f$ is $|f| = \sum_{(s,v)\in E} f(s,v) - \sum_{(v,s)\in E} f(v,s)$ (net flow out of $s$).

An $s$-$t$ cut $(S,T)$ is a partition $V = S \cup T$ with $s \in S$ and $t \in T$. The capacity of the cut is $\text{cap}(S,T) = \sum_{u \in S,\, v \in T,\, (u,v)\in E} c(u,v)$ (sum of forward edge capacities; backward edges do not count).

Theorem 6.1.3 — Weak Duality

For any flow $f$ and any $s$-$t$ cut $(S,T)$: $|f| \le \text{cap}(S,T)$. Proof: $|f|$ equals the net flow across the cut (by telescoping conservation), which is at most the sum of forward edge capacities = $\text{cap}(S,T)$.

Theorem 6.1.4 — Max-Flow Min-Cut (Ford-Fulkerson, 1956)

The following are equivalent for a flow $f$:

  1. $f$ is a maximum flow.
  2. There is no augmenting path from $s$ to $t$ in the residual graph $G_f$.
  3. There exists an $s$-$t$ cut $(S,T)$ with $|f| = \text{cap}(S,T)$.

Consequently, $\max_f |f| = \min_{(S,T)} \text{cap}(S,T)$.

Proof Sketch

$(1)\Rightarrow(2)$: If an augmenting path exists, we can increase flow, contradicting maximality. $(2)\Rightarrow(3)$: Let $S$ = vertices reachable from $s$ in $G_f$. Then $t \notin S$ (no augmenting path), so $(S,T=V\setminus S)$ is an $s$-$t$ cut. For every $(u,v)$ with $u\in S$, $v\in T$: $f(u,v)=c(u,v)$ (else $v$ would be reachable). For every $(v,u)$ with $v\in T$, $u\in S$: $f(v,u)=0$. Thus $|f|=\text{cap}(S,T)$. $(3)\Rightarrow(1)$: By weak duality, no flow can exceed $\text{cap}(S,T) = |f|$. $\square$

Remark 6.1.5 — Integrality Theorem

If all capacities are integers, there exists a maximum flow that is integral (all flow values are integers). This follows because Ford-Fulkerson with integer capacities always augments by a positive integer, and terminates at a maximum flow.
