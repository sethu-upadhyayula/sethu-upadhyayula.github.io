5.1 Inner Products and Norms

Definition - Inner Product

Let $V$ be a vector space over $\mathbb{F}$ ($=\mathbb{R}$ or $\mathbb{C}$). An inner product is a map $\langle\cdot,\cdot\rangle:V\times V\to\mathbb{F}$ satisfying:

  1. Conjugate symmetry: $\langle u,v\rangle=\overline{\langle v,u\rangle}$ (for $\mathbb{F}=\mathbb{R}$ this is just symmetry).
  2. Linearity in the first argument: $\langle\alpha u+\beta w,v\rangle=\alpha\langle u,v\rangle+\beta\langle w,v\rangle$.
  3. Positive definiteness: $\langle v,v\rangle\ge0$ with equality if and only if $v=\mathbf{0}$.

A vector space equipped with an inner product is called an inner product space.

Example - Standard Inner Products

Euclidean space $\mathbb{R}^n$: $\langle x,y\rangle=\sum_{i=1}^n x_iy_i=x^Ty$.

Complex space $\mathbb{C}^n$: $\langle x,y\rangle=\sum_{i=1}^n x_i\overline{y_i}=x^*y$ (where $x^*$ is the conjugate transpose).

Function space $C[a,b]$: $\langle f,g\rangle=\int_a^b f(x)g(x)\,dx$. This is an inner product on the space of continuous functions on $[a,b]$, used extensively in Fourier analysis.

Definition - Norm Induced by an Inner Product

The norm (length) induced by the inner product is: $$\|v\| = \sqrt{\langle v,v\rangle}.$$ In $\mathbb{R}^n$ with the standard inner product, $\|x\|=\sqrt{x_1^2+\cdots+x_n^2}$ is the Euclidean length. The angle $\theta$ between nonzero vectors $u,v$ is defined by $\cos\theta=\langle u,v\rangle/(\|u\|\|v\|)$.

Theorem - Cauchy–Schwarz Inequality

For all $u,v\in V$:

$$|\langle u,v\rangle|\le\|u\|\,\|v\|.$$

Equality holds if and only if $u$ and $v$ are linearly dependent (one is a scalar multiple of the other).

Proof - Cauchy–Schwarz

If $v=\mathbf{0}$ both sides are zero. Assume $v\neq\mathbf{0}$. For any $t\in\mathbb{R}$, consider:

$$0\le\|u-tv\|^2=\langle u-tv,u-tv\rangle=\|u\|^2-2t\langle u,v\rangle+t^2\|v\|^2.$$

This quadratic in $t$ is non-negative, so its discriminant satisfies $\Delta\le0$:

$$4\langle u,v\rangle^2-4\|u\|^2\|v\|^2\le0\implies|\langle u,v\rangle|\le\|u\|\|v\|.$$

$\square$

Theorem - Triangle Inequality and Parallelogram Law

Triangle inequality: $\|u+v\|\le\|u\|+\|v\|$ (follows from Cauchy–Schwarz).

Parallelogram law: $$\|u+v\|^2+\|u-v\|^2=2\|u\|^2+2\|v\|^2.$$ This identity characterises norms that come from an inner product: a norm satisfies the parallelogram law if and only if it is induced by some inner product.
