3. Determinants

The determinant is a scalar function on square matrices that encodes whether a matrix is invertible, measures signed volume scaling, and appears throughout advanced mathematics — in eigenvalue computations, change-of-variables in integration, and algebraic geometry.

Overview — Chapter 3
  • 3.1 Definition — the axiomatic (multilinear, alternating, normalized) definition and the Leibniz formula.
  • 3.2 Properties — multiplicativity, effect of row operations, and the invertibility criterion.
  • 3.3 Cofactor Expansion — the recursive formula, the adjugate matrix, and an explicit inverse formula.
