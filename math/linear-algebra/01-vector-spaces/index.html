1. Vector Spaces

A vector space is one of the most fundamental structures in mathematics. In this chapter we make the notion of "vectors" completely abstract, stripping away coordinates and working purely from axioms. This axiomatic viewpoint unifies $\mathbb{R}^n$, spaces of matrices, spaces of polynomials, and spaces of functions under a single framework.

Overview — Chapter 1

We cover four core ideas in sequence:

  • 1.1 Definition and Examples — the eight axioms and a menagerie of spaces that satisfy them.
  • 1.2 Subspaces — subsets that inherit the vector space structure, together with span and sum.
  • 1.3 Linear Independence and Basis — the minimal, non-redundant generating sets.
  • 1.4 Dimension — the unique size of any basis, and the rank-nullity theorem.
Remark

Throughout these notes the scalar field is $\mathbb{R}$ unless otherwise stated. All definitions extend verbatim to $\mathbb{C}$, and we note the complex case where it matters (especially in Chapters 4 and 6).
