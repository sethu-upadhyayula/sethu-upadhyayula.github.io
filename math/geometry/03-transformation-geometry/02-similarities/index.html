3.2 Similarities

A similarity transformation scales all distances by a constant factor, preserving shape but not necessarily size. The group of similarities extends the isometry group by allowing uniform scaling.

Definition 3.2.1 — Similarity

A bijection $f:\mathbb{R}^n \to \mathbb{R}^n$ is a similarity with ratio $k > 0$ if $d(f(x),f(y)) = k\cdot d(x,y)$ for all $x,y$. An isometry is a similarity with $k=1$.

Types of Similarities

Dilation (Homothety)

A dilation with centre $O$ and ratio $k$ maps $\mathbf{x}$ to: $$H_{O,k}(\mathbf{x}) = O + k(\mathbf{x} - O).$$ For $k>0$ it scales away from $O$; for $k<0$ it also reflects through $O$. The unique fixed point is $O$ (for $k\neq 1$).

Spiral Similarity

A spiral similarity is a composition of a rotation and a dilation about the same centre. In the complex plane: $z \mapsto az + b$ with $|a| = k$ (ratio) and $\arg(a) = \theta$ (angle).

Similar Triangles

Theorem 3.2.1 — AA Criterion

Two triangles with two pairs of equal corresponding angles are similar. (The third pair is then also equal, by the angle sum theorem.)

Theorem 3.2.2 — Proportional Sides

If $\triangle ABC \sim \triangle DEF$ with ratio $k$, then corresponding sides satisfy $DE/AB = EF/BC = FD/CA = k$. Areas scale as $k^2$.

Applications

Geometric Mean via Right Triangle

Given segments of lengths $a$ and $b$, their geometric mean $\sqrt{ab}$ is constructible. Place $a$ and $b$ end-to-end on a diameter; erect a semicircle; the altitude from the join point to the semicircle has length $\sqrt{ab}$. This follows from the altitude-on-hypotenuse theorem: in a right triangle, the altitude to the hypotenuse is the geometric mean of the two segments it creates.

Cross-Ratio and Möbius Transformations

The cross-ratio of four collinear points $(A,B;C,D) = (AC/CB)/(AD/DB)$ is preserved by similarities (and more generally by projective maps). Möbius transformations $z \mapsto (az+b)/(cz+d)$ of $\hat{\mathbb{C}}$ preserve cross-ratio.
