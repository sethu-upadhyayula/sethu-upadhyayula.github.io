7.1 Short Rate Models

Short rate models specify dynamics for the instantaneous risk-free rate $r_t$. Bond prices are then recovered as conditional expectations of the bank account discount factor.

Bond Pricing via Short Rate

Definition - Bond Price

Under the risk-neutral measure $Q$: $$P(t,T) = E^Q\!\left[\exp\!\left(-\int_t^T r_s\,ds\right)\bigg|\mathcal{F}_t\right].$$ The short rate $r_t$ evolves according to a specified SDE. For affine models, the bond price takes the form $P(t,T) = e^{A(t,T) - B(t,T)r_t}$ with deterministic functions $A$, $B$ satisfying Riccati ODEs.

Vasicek Model

Definition - Vasicek Model

$$dr_t = \kappa(\theta - r_t)\,dt + \sigma\,dW_t.$$ Mean-reverting Ornstein-Uhlenbeck process; long-run level $\theta$, speed of mean reversion $\kappa$, vol $\sigma$.

Explicit solution: $r_t = r_0\,e^{-\kappa t} + \theta(1-e^{-\kappa t}) + \sigma\int_0^t e^{-\kappa(t-s)}\,dW_s$. The rate is normally distributed (can go negative - a limitation).

Affine bond price: $P(t,T) = e^{A(\tau) - B(\tau)r_t}$ where $\tau = T-t$, $$B(\tau) = \frac{1-e^{-\kappa\tau}}{\kappa}, \quad A(\tau) = \left(\theta - \frac{\sigma^2}{2\kappa^2}\right)(B(\tau)-\tau) - \frac{\sigma^2 B(\tau)^2}{4\kappa}.$$

CIR Model

Definition - Cox-Ingersoll-Ross (CIR) Model

$$dr_t = \kappa(\theta - r_t)\,dt + \sigma\sqrt{r_t}\,dW_t.$$ The $\sqrt{r_t}$ diffusion ensures $r_t \ge 0$ provided $2\kappa\theta \ge \sigma^2$ (Feller condition). The distribution of $r_t$ is non-central chi-squared. Affine bond price with similar $A(\tau), B(\tau)$ to Vasicek but involving exponential and hyperbolic functions. Closed-form cap/floor formulas exist.

Hull-White Extended Vasicek

Definition - Hull-White Model

$$dr_t = (\theta(t) - \kappa r_t)\,dt + \sigma\,dW_t.$$ The time-dependent $\theta(t)$ is calibrated to fit the observed initial yield curve exactly. Since $\theta(t)$ can be computed analytically from the market discount curve, Hull-White is a no-arbitrage model that prices plain-vanilla bonds correctly by construction. European bond options and caps/floors have closed-form prices.
