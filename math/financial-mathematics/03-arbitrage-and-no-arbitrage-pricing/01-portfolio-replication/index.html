3.1 Portfolio Replication

A derivative can be priced by constructing a portfolio of basic assets that replicates its payoff in every state of the world. The no-arbitrage price of the derivative then equals the cost of the replicating portfolio.

Law of One Price

Theorem — Law of One Price

If two self-financing portfolios have identical cashflows in all states of the world at all future dates, they must have the same value at all times (including today). If they had different values, an investor could buy the cheaper and sell the more expensive, earning a riskless profit — an arbitrage.

Types of Arbitrage

Definition — Arbitrage

A type I arbitrage is a portfolio with negative cost today and non-negative payoff at all future times. A type II arbitrage is a portfolio with zero cost today, non-negative payoff with probability 1, and strictly positive payoff with positive probability. A market is arbitrage-free if neither type exists.

Forward Pricing: Cash-and-Carry

Theorem — Forward Price via No-Arbitrage

Consider a forward contract on an asset $S$ with maturity $T$, continuous dividend yield $q$, and continuously compounded risk-free rate $r$. The no-arbitrage forward price is: $$F = S_0\,e^{(r-q)T}.$$

Proof (cash-and-carry): Suppose $F > S_0 e^{(r-q)T}$. An arbitrageur: (1) enters the forward (sell $F$ at $T$, zero cost); (2) borrows $S_0$ at rate $r$; (3) buys asset at $S_0$, reinvesting dividends so holding $e^{qT}$ units at $T$; (4) delivers at $T$ to receive $F$, repays $S_0 e^{rT}$. Net profit $= F - S_0 e^{(r-q)T} > 0$.

Similarly if $F < S_0 e^{(r-q)T}$ (reverse cash-and-carry). Therefore $F = S_0 e^{(r-q)T}$ exactly.

Definition — FX Forward and Covered Interest Parity

For an FX forward (domestic rate $r_d$, foreign rate $r_f$, spot rate $X_0$ domestic per foreign): $$F = X_0\,e^{(r_d - r_f)T}.$$ This is covered interest parity (CIP). Deviations from CIP represent a risk-free profit for any entity that can borrow in both currencies at the rates $r_d$, $r_f$ simultaneously — typically only the largest banks.
