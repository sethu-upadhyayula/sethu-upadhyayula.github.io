6.2 Martingale Pricing

Martingale pricing provides a unified framework for valuing any contingent claim: the discounted price process is a $Q$-martingale, and the value of any replicable claim is a conditional expectation under $Q$.

Martingale Representation Theorem

Theorem - Martingale Representation Theorem

Let $M_t$ be a $Q$-martingale with respect to the Brownian filtration $(\mathcal{F}_t)$. Then there exists an adapted process $H_t$ such that: $$M_t = M_0 + \int_0^t H_s\,d\widetilde{W}_s.$$ Every $Q$-martingale is a stochastic integral with respect to the $Q$-Brownian motion. In the Black-Scholes model, this means every contingent claim is replicable: the market is complete.

Self-Financing Replication

Definition - Self-Financing Strategy and Replication

The replicating strategy for a claim $V_T$ is found as follows. The discounted claim $\widetilde{V}_t = e^{-rt}V_t$ is a $Q$-martingale: $$\widetilde{V}_t = E^Q[\widetilde{V}_T|\mathcal{F}_t].$$ By the MRT, $d\widetilde{V}_t = H_t\,d\widetilde{W}_t$ for some $H_t$. The delta (stock holding) is: $$\Delta_t = \frac{H_t}{\sigma \widetilde{S}_t}.$$

The bond holding is then $\psi_t = (\widetilde{V}_t - \Delta_t\widetilde{S}_t)$. The strategy $(\Delta_t, \psi_t)$ is self-financing (no external cash flows) and replicates $V_T$ exactly.

Path-Dependent Options and Monte Carlo

Remark - Path-Dependent Payoffs

For path-dependent claims such as Asian options ($g = (\bar{S} - K)^+$, $\bar{S} = \frac{1}{n}\sum_{i}S_{t_i}$) or barrier options, the pricing formula extends to: $$V_0 = e^{-rT}E^Q[g(S_{t_1},\ldots,S_{t_n})].$$ When no closed form exists, this expectation is evaluated by Monte Carlo simulation under $Q$: simulate many paths of $S$ using $dS = rS\,dt + \sigma S\,d\widetilde{W}$, compute $g$ for each path, and average.
