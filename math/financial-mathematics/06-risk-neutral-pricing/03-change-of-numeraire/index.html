6.3 Change of Numeraire

By choosing the numeraire (the unit of account) optimally, complex expectations can be simplified. The $T$-forward measure makes the forward price a martingale; the swap measure makes swaption pricing tractable.

General Numeraire Change

Theorem — Change of Numeraire

Let $N_t > 0$ be a strictly positive $Q$-martingale (numeraire). Define the numeraire measure $Q^N$ by: $$\left.\frac{dQ^N}{dQ}\right|_{\mathcal{F}_T} = \frac{N_T}{N_0\,B_T}, \quad B_T = e^{rT}.$$ Under $Q^N$, the ratio $V_t/N_t$ is a $Q^N$-martingale for any tradeable asset $V_t$. The pricing formula becomes: $$V_0 = N_0\,E^{Q^N}\!\left[\frac{V_T}{N_T}\right].$$

This is useful whenever the claim payoff, when denominated in units of the numeraire $N$, has a tractable distribution.

$T$-Forward Measure

Definition — $T$-Forward Measure

The $T$-forward measure $Q^T$ uses the zero-coupon bond $P(t,T)$ as numeraire. Under $Q^T$: $$V_0 = P(0,T)\,E^{Q^T}[V_T].$$ The forward price $F(0,T) = E^{Q^T}[S_T]$ is a $Q^T$-martingale. For a European call this gives Black's formula directly, since $S_T/P(0,T)$ has a log-normal distribution under $Q^T$ with vol equal to the forward vol. Caplet pricing under the $T$-forward measure eliminates the need to specify interest rate dynamics for the discounting.

Swap Measure and Black's Swaption Formula

Definition — Swap Measure

For a payer swaption (option to enter a swap paying fixed $K$, receiving floating, settlement dates $T_1 < \cdots < T_n$), use the annuity numeraire: $$A(t) = \sum_{i=1}^n \delta_i\,P(t,T_i),$$ where $\delta_i$ are day-count fractions. Under the swap measure} $Q^A$, the forward swap rate $F_{\text{swap}}(t) = (P(t,T_0)-P(t,T_n))/A(t)$ is a $Q^A$-martingale.

Black's swaption formula: If $F_{\text{swap}}$ follows Black dynamics (log-normal with vol $\sigma_{\text{sw}}$ under $Q^A$): $$V_0^{\text{payer}} = A(0)\bigl[F_{\text{swap}}(0)\,N(d_1) - K\,N(d_2)\bigr],$$ with $d_1 = [\ln(F/K) + \tfrac{1}{2}\sigma_{\text{sw}}^2 T_0]/(\sigma_{\text{sw}}\sqrt{T_0})$, $d_2 = d_1 - \sigma_{\text{sw}}\sqrt{T_0}$. The annuity $A(0)$ plays the role of the discount factor.
