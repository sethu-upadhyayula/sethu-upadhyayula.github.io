6.1 Risk-Neutral Measure

The risk-neutral measure $Q$ is the equivalent probability measure under which all discounted prices are martingales. Its construction relies on the Girsanov theorem, which shows how changing the drift of a Brownian motion corresponds to a change of measure.

Market Price of Risk

Definition - Sharpe Ratio and Market Price of Risk

Under $P$: $dS = \mu S\,dt + \sigma S\,dW_t$. The market price of risk (Sharpe ratio) is $\theta = (\mu - r)/\sigma$. It measures the excess return per unit of volatility.

Girsanov's Theorem

Theorem - Girsanov's Theorem

Define the Radon-Nikodym derivative: $$\left.\frac{dQ}{dP}\right|_{\mathcal{F}_T} = Z_T = e^{-\theta W_T - \frac{1}{2}\theta^2 T}.$$ Then $Q \sim P$ (equivalent measures) and the process $$\widetilde{W}_t = W_t + \theta t$$ is a standard $Q$-Brownian motion.

Under $Q$: $dS = rS\,dt + \sigma S\,d\widetilde{W}_t$. The drift changes from $\mu$ to $r$; volatility $\sigma$ is unchanged. The discounted price $\widetilde{S}_t = e^{-rt}S_t$ satisfies $d\widetilde{S}_t = \sigma\widetilde{S}_t\,d\widetilde{W}_t$, a driftless (martingale) SDE.

Risk-Neutral Pricing Formula

Theorem - Pricing under $Q$

The no-arbitrage price of a European claim $V_T = g(S_T)$ at time 0 is: $$V_0 = e^{-rT}\,E^Q[g(S_T)],$$ where $E^Q$ denotes expectation under $Q$. Under $Q$, $S_T = S_0\,e^{(r-\frac{1}{2}\sigma^2)T + \sigma\widetilde{W}_T}$, which is log-normally distributed regardless of the real-world drift $\mu$. This is why option prices depend on $\sigma$ but not on $\mu$.
