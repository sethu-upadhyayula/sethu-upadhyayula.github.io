1.2 Annuities and Perpetuities

An annuity is a finite stream of equal periodic payments; a perpetuity is an infinite stream. Closed-form formulas for their present values are among the most practically useful results in finance.

Ordinary Annuity

Definition — Ordinary Annuity

An ordinary annuity pays $C$ at the end of each period for $n$ periods, with per-period discount rate $r$. Its present value is $$PV = C \sum_{k=1}^n \frac{1}{(1+r)^k} = \frac{C}{r}\left[1 - (1+r)^{-n}\right].$$

An annuity due pays at the beginning of each period (shifted one period earlier): $$PV_{\text{due}} = \frac{C}{r}\left[1-(1+r)^{-n}\right]\cdot(1+r).$$

Growing Annuity

Definition — Growing Annuity

If payments grow at rate $g$ per period (first payment $C$, at $t=1$): $$PV = \frac{C}{r-g}\left[1 - \left(\frac{1+g}{1+r}\right)^n\right], \quad r \ne g.$$ If $r = g$: $PV = Cn/(1+r)$.

Perpetuity and Growing Perpetuity

Definition — Perpetuity

A perpetuity pays $C$ at the end of each period forever. Taking $n\to\infty$ in the annuity formula (assuming $r>0$): $$PV = \frac{C}{r}.$$

A growing perpetuity with growth rate $g < r$: $$PV = \frac{C}{r - g}.$$ This is the Gordon Growth Model for dividend discount valuation.

Loan Amortisation

Definition — Amortising Loan

A loan of principal $L$ is repaid via $n$ equal monthly payments $M$ at monthly rate $r_m = r/12$ (annual rate $r$). Setting $PV = L$ and solving: $$M = \frac{L\,r_m}{1-(1+r_m)^{-n}}.$$

At each period $k$, the outstanding balance $B_k$, interest component $I_k$, and principal repaid $P_k$ are: $$B_k = M\,\frac{1-(1+r_m)^{-(n-k)}}{r_m}, \quad I_k = r_m B_{k-1}, \quad P_k = M - I_k.$$

Example — 25-Year Mortgage

Loan $L = £200{,}000$, annual rate $r = 5\%$, $n = 300$ monthly payments, $r_m = 0.05/12 \approx 0.004167$.

Monthly payment: $M = 200{,}000 \times 0.004167 / [1-(1.004167)^{-300}] \approx £1{,}169.18$.

Total payments: $300 \times 1{,}169.18 = £350{,}754$, of which £150,754 is interest.

After 5 years (60 payments), outstanding balance: $B_{60} = 1{,}169.18 \times [1-(1.004167)^{-240}]/0.004167 \approx £180{,}854$. Only £19,146 principal has been repaid in the first 5 years — the early years are predominantly interest.
