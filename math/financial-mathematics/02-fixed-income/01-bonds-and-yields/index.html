2.1 Bonds and Yields

A bond is a debt instrument that promises to pay a fixed coupon periodically and return the principal (face value) at maturity. Bond pricing reduces to discounting these cashflows, and the yield-to-maturity is the single discount rate that makes this calculation consistent with the market price.

Coupon Bond Pricing

Definition - Coupon Bond Price and YTM

A bond with annual coupon $C$, face value $F$, and $n$ periods to maturity is priced by discounting all cashflows at the yield-to-maturity $y$: $$P = \sum_{t=1}^{n} \frac{C}{(1+y)^t} + \frac{F}{(1+y)^n} = \frac{C}{y}\left[1-(1+y)^{-n}\right] + F(1+y)^{-n}.$$

The YTM $y$ is found numerically (Newton's method) from the observed price $P$. Relationship to face value:

  • If $C/F = y$: bond trades at par ($P = F$).
  • If $C/F > y$: bond trades at a premium ($P > F$).
  • If $C/F < y$: bond trades at a discount ($P < F$).

Zero-Coupon Bonds and Spot Rates

Definition - Zero-Coupon Bond and Spot Rate

A zero-coupon bond (ZCB) pays only face value $F$ at maturity $T$. Its price: $$P(0,T) = F\,e^{-z_T T}$$ defines the continuously compounded spot rate $z_T$ for maturity $T$. The function $T \mapsto z_T$ is the zero-coupon yield curve or term structure of interest rates.

Bootstrapping Spot Rates

Definition - Bootstrapping Algorithm

Given coupon bond prices for maturities $T_1 < T_2 < \cdots$, extract spot rates $z_{T_k}$ sequentially. For the first maturity $T_1$ (short enough that any coupon structure is equivalent to a ZCB by market convention): $z_{T_1} = -\ln(P_1/F)/T_1$.

For each subsequent maturity $T_k$, the coupon bond price is $$P_k = \sum_{j=1}^{k-1} C_k e^{-z_{T_j}T_j} + (C_k + F)e^{-z_{T_k}T_k}.$$ The first $k-1$ terms are known from previous steps; solve for $z_{T_k}$. This strips out a complete zero-coupon yield curve from coupon bond prices.

Forward Rates

Definition - Forward Rate

The forward rate $f(S,T)$ is the rate agreed today for borrowing/lending over $[S,T]$ in the future. By no-arbitrage: $$f(S,T) = \frac{z_T T - z_S S}{T - S}.$$ The instantaneous forward rate is $f(t) = \lim_{T\to t}f(t,T) = z_t + t\,\partial z_t/\partial t$. A forward rate agreement (FRA) is a derivative that locks in $f(S,T)$ with zero initial cost by construction.
