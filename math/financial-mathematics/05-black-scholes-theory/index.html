5. Black-Scholes Theory

The Black-Scholes model (1973) revolutionised quantitative finance by providing the first rigorous continuous-time framework for option pricing. Assuming geometric Brownian motion for the stock and a constant risk-free rate, it yields an explicit closed-form formula for European option prices and a complete description of the replicating hedge through the Greeks.
