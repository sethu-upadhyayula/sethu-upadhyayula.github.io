Chapter 6: Fields and Galois Theory

Galois theory, one of the crowning achievements of nineteenth-century mathematics, connects field extensions to group theory. It explains precisely which polynomial equations can be solved by radicals - and, in particular, why the general quintic cannot - by associating to every field extension a group that encodes its symmetries.
