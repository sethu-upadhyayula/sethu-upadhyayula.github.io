2.2 Normal Subgroups and Quotient Groups

Normal subgroups are exactly the subgroups that arise as kernels of homomorphisms. They allow us to form quotient groups - one of the most powerful constructions in algebra.

Definition 2.2.1 - Normal Subgroup

A subgroup $N \le G$ is normal, written $N \trianglelefteq G$, if any of the following equivalent conditions holds:

  1. $gNg^{-1} = N$ for all $g \in G$.
  2. $gN = Ng$ for all $g \in G$ (left and right cosets coincide).
  3. $gng^{-1} \in N$ for all $g \in G$, $n \in N$.

Every subgroup of an abelian group is normal.

Example 2.2.2
  • $A_n \trianglelefteq S_n$: since $\text{sgn}$ is a homomorphism and $A_n = \ker(\text{sgn})$.
  • $SL_n(\mathbb{R}) \trianglelefteq GL_n(\mathbb{R})$: kernel of $\det$.
  • The centre $Z(G) = \{z \in G : zg = gz \ \forall g \in G\} \trianglelefteq G$.
  • The commutator subgroup $[G,G] = \langle aba^{-1}b^{-1} : a,b \in G \rangle \trianglelefteq G$.

Quotient Groups

Definition 2.2.3 - Quotient Group

If $N \trianglelefteq G$, the quotient group (or factor group) is: $$G/N = \{gN : g \in G\}$$ with multiplication $(aN)(bN) = (ab)N$.

This operation is well-defined if and only if $N$ is normal. The identity is $eN = N$, and $(gN)^{-1} = g^{-1}N$. We have $|G/N| = [G:N] = |G|/|N|$ when $G$ is finite.

Well-Definedness of Quotient Multiplication

We need: if $aN = a'N$ and $bN = b'N$, then $(ab)N = (a'b')N$. Since $a' = an$ and $b' = bm$ for some $n, m \in N$: $$a'b' = (an)(bm) = ab(b^{-1}nb)m.$$ Since $N \trianglelefteq G$, $b^{-1}nb \in N$, so $b^{-1}nb \cdot m \in N$, giving $a'b' \in (ab)N$, i.e. $(a'b')N = (ab)N$. $\square$

Example 2.2.4 - Key Quotient Groups
  • $\mathbb{Z}/n\mathbb{Z} = \mathbb{Z}/(n\mathbb{Z})$: the integers modulo $n$, quotient of $(\mathbb{Z},+)$ by the normal subgroup $n\mathbb{Z}$.
  • $\mathbb{R}/\mathbb{Z} \cong S^1$ (the circle group): cosets are $\{x + \mathbb{Z}\}$, isomorphic to $\{e^{2\pi i x}\}$ via $x + \mathbb{Z} \mapsto e^{2\pi i x}$.
  • $S_n/A_n \cong \mathbb{Z}/2\mathbb{Z}$: the quotient of $S_n$ by even permutations.
Theorem 2.2.5 - Natural Projection

The map $\pi : G \to G/N$ defined by $\pi(g) = gN$ is a surjective group homomorphism (the natural projection or quotient map) with $\ker\pi = N$.

Remark - Abelianisation

The commutator subgroup $[G,G]$ is the smallest normal subgroup $N$ such that $G/N$ is abelian. The quotient $G/[G,G]$ is called the abelianisation of $G$ and captures the "abelian part" of $G$. For $S_n$: $[S_n, S_n] = A_n$ and $S_n/A_n \cong \mathbb{Z}/2\mathbb{Z}$.
