2.3 Isomorphism Theorems

The isomorphism theorems relate homomorphisms, normal subgroups, and quotient groups. They are among the most frequently cited results in algebra.

Theorem 2.3.1 - First Isomorphism Theorem

Let $\phi : G \to H$ be a group homomorphism. Then: $$G / \ker\phi \cong \text{im}\,\phi.$$ The isomorphism is given by $\bar{\phi} : g\ker\phi \mapsto \phi(g)$.

Proof

Let $K = \ker\phi$. Define $\bar{\phi} : G/K \to \text{im}\,\phi$ by $\bar{\phi}(gK) = \phi(g)$.

Well-defined: if $gK = g'K$ then $g^{-1}g' \in K$ so $\phi(g^{-1}g') = e_H$, giving $\phi(g) = \phi(g')$.

Homomorphism: $\bar{\phi}((gK)(hK)) = \bar{\phi}((gh)K) = \phi(gh) = \phi(g)\phi(h) = \bar{\phi}(gK)\bar{\phi}(hK)$.

Injective: if $\bar{\phi}(gK) = e_H$ then $\phi(g) = e_H$ so $g \in K$, giving $gK = K = e_{G/K}$.

Surjective: for any $\phi(g) \in \text{im}\,\phi$, $\bar{\phi}(gK) = \phi(g)$. $\square$

Example

Apply to $\phi : \mathbb{Z} \to \mathbb{Z}/n\mathbb{Z}$, $\phi(k) = [k]$: $\ker\phi = n\mathbb{Z}$ and $\text{im}\,\phi = \mathbb{Z}/n\mathbb{Z}$, so $\mathbb{Z}/(n\mathbb{Z}) \cong \mathbb{Z}/n\mathbb{Z}$. Consistent by construction.

Theorem 2.3.2 - Second Isomorphism Theorem

Let $H \le G$ and $N \trianglelefteq G$. Then $HN \le G$, $H \cap N \trianglelefteq H$, and: $$(HN)/N \cong H/(H \cap N).$$

Theorem 2.3.3 - Third Isomorphism Theorem

Let $K \trianglelefteq H \trianglelefteq G$ (with $K \trianglelefteq G$ as well). Then: $$(G/K)\,/\,(H/K) \cong G/H.$$

This says "dividing out in stages gives the same result as dividing out all at once."

Example of Third Theorem

Take $G = \mathbb{Z}$, $H = 2\mathbb{Z}$, $K = 6\mathbb{Z}$. Then $(\mathbb{Z}/6\mathbb{Z}) / (2\mathbb{Z}/6\mathbb{Z}) \cong \mathbb{Z}/2\mathbb{Z}$. Check: $\mathbb{Z}/6\mathbb{Z}$ has order 6, $2\mathbb{Z}/6\mathbb{Z} \cong \mathbb{Z}/3\mathbb{Z}$ has order 3, and the quotient has order $6/3 = 2$. Consistent with $\mathbb{Z}/2\mathbb{Z}$.

Theorem 2.3.4 - Correspondence Theorem (Fourth Isomorphism Theorem)

Let $N \trianglelefteq G$ and $\pi : G \to G/N$ the natural projection. There is an order-preserving bijection: $$\{\text{subgroups of } G \text{ containing } N\} \leftrightarrow \{\text{subgroups of } G/N\}$$ given by $H \mapsto H/N$, with inverse $\bar{H} \mapsto \pi^{-1}(\bar{H})$. Moreover, $H \trianglelefteq G$ if and only if $H/N \trianglelefteq G/N$.

Remark

The correspondence theorem is essential for understanding the subgroup lattice of $G/N$. For example, the subgroups of $\mathbb{Z}/12\mathbb{Z}$ correspond exactly to subgroups of $\mathbb{Z}$ containing $12\mathbb{Z}$, i.e. $d\mathbb{Z}$ for $d \mid 12$: they are $\{[0]\}$, $\langle[2]\rangle$, $\langle[3]\rangle$, $\langle[4]\rangle$, $\langle[6]\rangle$, $\mathbb{Z}/12\mathbb{Z}$.
