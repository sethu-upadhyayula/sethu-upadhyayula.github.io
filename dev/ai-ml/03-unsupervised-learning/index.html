3. Unsupervised Learning

Unsupervised learning works with data that carries no labels at all — rather than learning a mapping to a known output (Chapter 2), it looks for structure or patterns that the data itself exhibits.

Clustering

Definition — Clustering

Clustering groups data points into clusters such that points within a cluster are more similar to each other than to points in other clusters. k-means, one of the simplest and most common clustering algorithms, alternates between assigning each point to its nearest of k cluster centers and recomputing each center as the average of the points assigned to it, repeating until the assignment stabilizes.

Dimensionality Reduction

Dimensionality reduction techniques compress data with many features down to a much smaller number of derived features, while preserving as much of the data's meaningful variation as possible — useful both for visualization (compressing to 2 or 3 dimensions for plotting) and as a preprocessing step before other techniques. Principal component analysis (PCA), the most common such technique, finds the directions of greatest variance in the data and projects onto the top few of them.

Anomaly Detection

Anomaly detection identifies data points that differ substantially from the great majority of the rest of the data — such as flagging an unusual transaction as potentially fraudulent — often built on top of a model (such as a clustering result above, or a density estimate) of what "normal" data looks like, flagging points that fit that model poorly.

Why Unsupervised Learning Is Harder to Evaluate

Remark — No Ground Truth

Because there are no correct labels to compare against, evaluating an unsupervised result is inherently less objective than evaluating a supervised prediction (Chapter 2) — "is this the correct clustering?" often has no single right answer, and practitioners typically fall back on proxy measures (such as how tightly clusters group together) or simply on whether the result is useful for the downstream task it was produced for.
